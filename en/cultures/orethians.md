# Orethians

*Where stone sings of war and justice*

## Origin and Cultural Profile

The Orethians are a mountain civilization, born around mining, metallurgy, and military strategy. They **value discipline, martial honor, and the continuity of blood and word**. Cities are carved into rock and defended by layered fortresses. Warrior families coexist with castes of artisans and miners in a rigorous balance.

## Identity Tags

"Guard with strength," "Swear by iron," "Become stone"

## Playable Archetypes

* Captain of the Black Steel, ready to betray for an inheritance
* Artisan of the Sacred Forge, in search of lost metal
* Militant orator of the Faith of Tudeshkhast
* Blind strategist, who sees beyond the mountain

## Internal Tensions and Fault Lines

* **Ritual war vs. total war**: divergences on how and when to fight
* **Cult of war vs. cult of memory**: Tudeshkhast and Shemshahva offer opposing visions
* **Rigid hierarchy vs. military meritocracy**: traditionalist clans clash with young generals
* **Dwindling resources**: the wealth of the mines grows ever less certain

## Symbols, Proverbs, Objects

* **Symbol**: the Broken Hammer, icon of implacable justice
* **Proverb**: "He who leaves the rock has no more name."
* **Sacred object**: the Oath Mask, worn only for binding vows

## Extended Profile

**Culture Name:** Orethian

**Basic Information:**

* **Origins:** The Orethians derive from ancient mountain peoples who inhabited the elevated regions of Marethar. Their culture evolved around mining and weapons production, making them one of the most militarized civilizations in the region.
* **Primary Language:** Orethian
* **Total Population:** 1,888,322

**Cultural Information:**

* **Core Traditions:** Orethian traditions are closely bound to mountain life and war. Military initiation ceremonies and festivals honoring war gods are central events. Orethian culture celebrates strength, courage, and loyalty.
* **Festivals and Celebrations:** The Festival of the Warrior, which honors military heroes and celebrates victories in battle, is one of the most important. Participants don ceremonial armor and take part in martial skill competitions. The Festival of the Forge is another key celebration, dedicated to craftsmen and their creations.
* **Social Structure:** Orethian society is strongly hierarchical, with a dominant military class and a rigid caste system. Tribal chiefs and warlords hold power, while craftsmen and miners are respected for their skills. Women may attain positions of command in both military and social domains.
* **Art and Architecture:** Orethian art is characterized by intricate metalwork, sculptures of heroes, and battle scenes. Architecture includes mountain fortresses, robust castles, and ingeniously constructed mines. Cities are often fortified and built in stone.

**Religious Information:**

* **Primary Religion:**
  * **Religion Name:** Faith of Tudeshkhast
  * **Supreme Deity:** Tudeshkhast, the Flying Sage of Destiny
  * **Description:** The Faith of Tudeshkhast is grounded in dualism and venerates Tudeshkhast as a symbol of balance between light and darkness, life and death. Rituals include offerings and prayers in dedicated temples situated on mountain summits.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 6,012,780
* **Minor Religions:**
  * **Religion Name:** Faith of Shemshahva
  * **Supreme Deity:** Shemshahva, the Brown Cobra
  * **Description:** The Faith of Shemshahva centers on ancestor veneration and the worship of Shemshahva. Rituals include offerings to ancestors and prayers for guidance and protection.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 1,372,268

**Technological Information:**

* **General Technology Level:** Medium-High
* **Key Technologies:** Advanced mining techniques, metalworking, military engineering, and fortification construction.
* **Notable Innovations:** The Orethians are known for the creation of high-quality weapons and armor, the use of advanced mining techniques, and the construction of complex mountain defense systems.
