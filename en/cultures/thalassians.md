# Thalassians

*Between the waves, the pact is law*

## Origin and Cultural Profile

Descended from coastal and island peoples, the Thalassians live amid sea currents and maritime trade. Their culture is founded on **practical collaboration, port efficiency, and the protection of the collective**, with a strong devotion to Thalaran, the Spirit of the Sea. Thalassian ports are open but carefully watched, and cities organized as networks rather than centers.

## Identity Tags

"Act together, swim together," "The sea does not forgive the ego," "Every sail has a duty"

## Playable Archetypes

* Harbor Master, accused of ritual corruption
* Deep-sea navigator who refuses every written route
* Boat captain devoted to Carthara, ready for anything for the cargo
* Protector of a coastal community threatened by a commercial alliance

## Internal Tensions and Fault Lines

* **Free trade vs. protection of routes**: what to share, and with whom?
* **Faith of Thalaran vs. Cult of Carthara**: spiritual protection or material prosperity?
* **Islanders vs. port-dwellers**: divergences between those who live on the sea and those who manage its economy
* **Ecological fragility**: fish stocks and tides are changing, and the sea does not forget

## Symbols, Proverbs, Objects

* **Symbol**: the Tidal Net, representation of the relationships between naval clans
* **Proverb**: "He who does not close the cargo, closes his own coffin."
* **Sacred object**: the Blessed Salt Plate, carried in the holds as a talisman

## Extended Profile

**Culture Name:** Thalassian

**Basic Information:**

* **Origins:** The Thalassians derive from ancient coastal peoples who inhabited the islands and shores of Marethar. Their culture evolved around maritime trade, navigation, and fishing, making them one of the region's naval powers.
* **Primary Language:** Thalassian
* **Total Population:** 3,611,967

**Cultural Information:**

* **Core Traditions:** Thalassian traditions are deeply rooted in seafaring life and trade. Sailing competitions, festivals honoring sailors, and celebrations of great discoveries are central to their culture. Thalassian society values nautical skill, commercial ingenuity, and cooperation.
* **Festivals and Celebrations:** The Festival of the Waves is the most important celebration, where coastal communities gather to feast with sailing races, fish banquets, and fireworks. Trade Day honors merchant traditions with open markets and cultural exchange.
* **Social Structure:** Thalassian society comprises a dominant merchant class, sailors, fishermen, and craftsmen. City-states are governed by councils of merchants or naval oligarchies. Women may hold significant roles in trade and city-state administration.
* **Art and Architecture:** Thalassian art is known for its intricate ship carvings, marine frescoes, and mosaics. Architecture includes well-planned harbors, lighthouses, warehouses, and temples dedicated to sea deities. Coastal cities are characterized by colorful houses and lively markets.

**Religious Information:**

* **Primary Religion:**
  * **Religion Name:** Spirits of Thalaran
  * **Supreme Deity:** Thalaran, Spirit of the Sea
  * **Description:** The Spirits of Thalaran venerate Thalaran as protector of seas and waters. Rituals include offerings of fish and precious objects cast into the sea to secure protection during voyages.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 6,621,114
* **Minor Religions:**
  * **Religion Name:** Cult of Carthara
  * **Supreme Deity:** Carthara, Protector of Merchants
  * **Description:** The Cult of Carthara worships Carthara as the patron deity of traders and maritime travelers. The faithful offer sacrifices and prayers to secure prosperity and protection.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 1,630,629

**Technological Information:**

* **General Technology Level:** High
* **Key Technologies:** Advanced shipbuilding techniques, navigation instruments, harbor architecture, and sea-trade methods.
* **Notable Innovations:** The Thalassians are known for the construction of fast and durable ships, the development of detailed nautical charts, and the creation of advanced lighthouses to guide vessels into harbor.
