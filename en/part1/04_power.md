## 🜨 Shades of Power

*Invisible structures that sustain Marethar*

In Marethar, power is not concentrated in a single place, nor does it always manifest as thrones, armies, or decrees. There exist **subtler forms of influence**, distributed along cultural, religious, economic, and academic axes. Some are visible; others have operated for centuries in shadow; still others blend with tradition or faith.

To understand the tensions that drive the continent, one must examine three of its most widespread and interwoven foundations: **religions, guilds, and networks of knowledge transmission**.

### Religions That Govern Peoples

In many regions of Marethar, religion **is not separate from political administration or jurisprudence**. Deities are not symbols: they are **active forces** within normative systems, civic rituals, cultural identities, and law.

#### Forms of Religious Influence

* In Aridonia, the **Sect of Shahin** establishes rites of passage, calendars, and in many cases ratifies tribal appointments.
* In Vallan and Orethia, the **shamanic faiths of the ancestors** influence access to military power and the validation of enterprises.
* In Linia, the **Faith of the Gods of Ralertis** supports the Senate in symbolic and patrimonial matters, but without direct executive power.
* In Thalassia, animistic faiths such as the **cult of Thalaran** govern merchant behavior, especially at sea.

#### Typical Conflicts

* **Uncertain jurisdiction**: who judges a sacrilegious crime? The temple or the tribunal?
* **Functional heresies**: minority cults emerge to address social needs unmet by official faiths.
* **Spontaneous syncretisms**: in border cities, rites contaminate one another and communities quarrel over which interpretation is "true."

> *Archival note: in numerous frontier villages between Premia and Ashania, the two religious calendars have merged into a single one — the "Cycle of Two Lights" — recognized by no authority.*

### Guilds and Orders as Supranational Structures

In Marethar, merchants' and artisans' guilds are not simple economic consortia. Some of them **wield powers comparable to those of magistracies**, influencing prices, quality standards, access to professions, and trade treaties.

#### Relevant Examples

* The **Merchants' Guild of Meshdad** controls the spice network, sets official prices, and mediates between cities in commercial war.
* The **Naval Builders' Corporation of Lysandria** defines who may launch ships, imposing extrajudicial sanctions as well.
* The **Artisans' Brotherhood of Vallan** has the power to block the use of unapproved materials, as occurred with "resonant silver."

Many guilds impose **initiation rites, oaths, and symbols of membership**, often unknown to outsiders. Their power is **cross-state**, and cities that try to curtail them often see economic crises or prolonged technical strikes arise.

#### Spiritual and Magical Orders

* **Religious orders** (such as the Druidic Orders of Dimilalica or the Shamans of Shemshahva) often perform functions parallel to those of academies.
* Some **magical orders** (e.g., the Order of Mages of Thalassia) impose codes of magical conduct and maintain registries of artifacts and practitioners.
* **Secret societies** (e.g., the Black Hand or the Brotherhood of Rashian Assassins) operate as occult powers, often linked to nobility or merchants.

> *Entry censored from the registers of Fahya:* "At the Council of Copper, it was an alchemist — not a general — who dictated the terms of the treaty."

### Oral Tradition, Academy, and the Power of Knowledge

Marethar is a continent where **the transmission of knowledge is not uniform**. In some regions, knowledge is oral and entrusted to elders, priests, or wanderers. In others, it is **academized, preserved in libraries and treatises**, often inaccessible to the uninitiated.

#### Examples and Tensions

* In Premia, the **College of Philosophers of Preiacoria** imposes linguistic and interpretive standards for historical texts.
* In Fahya, the **Magical University** regulates who may practice and teach magic — and which formulae are forbidden.
* In Aridonia and Naharim, **legends are transmitted in poetic form**, often with contradictory local variants.

#### Recurring Themes

* **Who has the right to write?** Some cultures never transcribe visions. Others ritualize them only after collective confirmation.
* **Who has the right to read?** Academies impose exams, oaths, censures. In many regions, knowledge is political property.
* **Which truths survive?** Guilds destroy competing recipes. Cults rewrite chronologies. Legends erase embarrassing facts.

> *Epigraph carved on a fragment of statue at Lurocedina:*
> "A forgotten truth is a permitted lie."
