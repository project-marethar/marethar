## 🝗 Magic, Alchemy, Knowledge

*Forces That Will Not Be Tamed*

In Marethar, knowledge is fragmented, contested, stratified. Magic, alchemy, and ancestral knowledge have never been a unified patrimony, nor accessible to all. Every culture has codified it according to its own needs; every order has sought to delimit it; every power has tried to bend it to its ends.
No form of knowledge is neutral. Every act of knowing is also a political act.

### The Four Paths of Arcane Power

Though terminology varies, four major axes are recognized along which magical practices unfold in Marethar. They are not mutually exclusive, but rarely coexist within the same individual or institution.

#### Elemental Magic

**Origin and spread**: It is the oldest, and in many regions the most widespread. Rooted in archaic cults and shamanic rituals, it is grounded in the symbolic and practical mastery of the natural elements: fire, water, earth, air.
**Practice**: It requires a direct relationship with the environment. Aridonian shamans command the ceremonial fire. The priests of Dor use water for purification. The druids of Dimilalica celebrate the seasonal winds and tectonic shifts.
**Tensions**: Some academic orders consider this form crude and uncontrollable. Some cities ban it, fearing its destructive use in vengeance rituals.

#### Divine Magic

**Origin and spread**: It derives from the invocation of deities or superior spirits. It is tightly bound to the local religious structure and the legitimacy of its officiants.
**Practice**: It passes through rituals, offerings, chants, prostrations. Only recognized devotees may access its powers. In Aridonia, the priestesses of Shahin heal with consecrated sap. In Orethia, the followers of Tudeshkhast use magic for rites of equilibrium.
**Tensions**: Religious authorities control access to rituals. Heresies (such as the Sect of the Victory Bull) are persecuted for "improper" use of magic. Some faiths compete for the same domain (e.g., over healing or visions).

#### Shamanic Magic

**Origin and spread**: It belongs to tribal and marginal cultures. Founded on trance, dreams, possession, dialogue with spirits and ancestors.
**Practice**: It is not learned in an academy. It is received through visions, rites of passage, and contacts with the invisible world. The shamans of Shemshahva, for example, pass down chants that alter the perception of time.
**Tensions**: It is considered unreliable by many authorities. In some cities it is tolerated as folklore; in others, suppressed as heresy or fraud. Yet its actual power is acknowledged even by those who fear it.

#### Alchemical Magic

**Origin and spread**: It is born in laboratories, academies, and among the guilds. It combines empirical, symbolic, and mystical knowledge.
**Practice**: It requires techniques, tools, knowledge of rare substances, and encoded ciphers. In Linia and Premia, alchemists work under contract for the élite, creating potions, explosives, seals, and arcane mechanisms.
**Tensions**: Its efficacy is feared as much as its opacity. Some confuse it with pure science; others see it as a social threat. Formulae are jealously guarded; fraud is frequent.

### Who Has Access to Power? At What Price?

Magic is not for everyone. It never has been.
In some cultures it is hereditary; in others, granted by cults; elsewhere, the fruit of study. But everywhere, its use implies a **material, social, or existential cost**.

#### Control Structures

* **Temples**: authorize or prohibit certain rituals. In some places, only consecrated priests may invoke.
* **Academies**: officially train mages, but impose economic and political entry filters. In Fahya, the Magical University has an entrance examination that only one in ten candidates passes.
* **Orders and guilds**: decide who may sell, teach, or inherit formulae. Transgressors may be pursued legally… or by other means.

#### Common Costs

* **Physical**: prolonged use may consume the body or the mind. Some practitioners manifest subtle mutations, spiritual hemorrhaging, progressive blindness.
* **Social**: many practitioners are feared, isolated, exiled. Some accept renown in exchange for marginalization.
* **Ritual**: some powers require offerings, sacrifices, isolation. Some rites must be performed alone; others, never twice in the same place.

> *Ritual note found in the College of Philosophers*:
> "Every formula has a price. If you do not know what it is, you are only late in paying it."

### Sites of Power, Concentration, and Anomaly

Some places in Marethar display anomalous concentrations of arcane energy. Sometimes they are considered **sacred or forbidden**. In other cases, they are **zones of conflict, study, or legend**.

#### Ritual Sites

* **The crypts of Shahin** at Nabad
* **The ceremonial clearings** of the Aurel Forest
* **The river sanctuaries** along the Dor, where the water is called "singing"

Spaces still active, tied to seasonal ceremonies, solstices, apparitions.

#### Laboratories and Centers of Knowledge

* **The isolated towers** of Linia's alchemists
* **The deep laboratories** of the university of Fahya
* **The chambers of forbidden substances** in Preiacoria

Here experiments are conducted, and *attempts made to map* power itself. Some of these places have been kept secret for centuries.

#### Ambiguous and Contested Sites

* The **submerged ruins** of Thalassia, visible only during reverse tides
* The **silent mines** of Vallan, from which metals that "make no sound" are extracted
* The **caves of the Desert of Suleiman**, which no map can fix twice

On these places there is no consensus: some consider them cursed, others sites of revelation. Scholars are divided between those who wish to isolate them and those who wish to exploit them.

#### Voices and Fragments

> "There is a room beneath the city of Allasia where the air never ages."
> "The recurring dreams of the Sect of the Victory Bull's devotees correspond to the map of a forgotten fortress."
> "An alembic left burning too long sang for three days in the tower of Premia."
