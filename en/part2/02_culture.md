## 🜄 Cultures of Marethar

*Peoples, memories, and systems of meaning*

### Aridonians

*Where life is resistance and memory is sand*

#### Origin and Cultural Profile

The Aridonians descend from nomadic tribes settled in the deserts and highlands of southern Marethar. Though today they also live in well-structured oasis cities, they **maintain a strong bond with ancestral practices**, in particular the cult of ancestors, clan honor, and the rhythms of the desert.

They are known for their **resilience, tribal structure, and deep communal sense**. Their architecture is mobile or semi-permanent, cities are founded around water sources, and life is measured by solar cycles, ritual migrations, and collective celebrations.

#### Identity Tags

"Honor the ancestors," "Survive together," "Never forget an offense"

#### Playable Archetypes

* Daughter of the Sand, ready to take her mother's place as keeper of stories
* Caravan warrior, seeking redemption after breaking an oath
* Former priest of Shahin, in exile after a heretical dream
* Young chieftain, fighting against the order of the assembled chiefs

#### Internal Tensions and Fault Lines

* **Tradition vs. innovation**: Some oases open to outside trade; others wish to maintain ritual purity.
* **Cult of Shahin vs. Golat Tradition**: the Sect of Shahin is dominant, but in border zones the shamanic cult of the Yellow Panther gains followers.
* **Role of women**: respected everywhere, but in some tribes they hold full political power; in others, only symbolic.
* **Clanism vs. federation**: the Council of Chiefs seeks unity, but the oldest clans want autonomy.

#### Symbols, Proverbs, Objects

* **Symbol**: the Flayed Tree, emblem of the life-death-regeneration cycle
* **Proverb**: "He who forgets his grandfather's name loses his shadow."
* **Sacred object**: the Clay Lion, simulacrum of courage used in the Sand Ceremonies

### Aurelians

*Descendants of the Empire, lovers of form and order*

#### Origin and Cultural Profile

The Aurelians sink their roots in a central empire that dominated Marethar centuries ago. Though fragmented over time, they preserve a strong **imperial, celebratory, and rationalist identity**.
Their culture is layered and ordered, with a clear class division, but with margins of social mobility through public service, military career, or the accumulation of wealth.

They are celebrated for their art, engineering, codification of law, and the organization of urban space. Their architecture blends monumentalism with functional precision.

#### Identity Tags

"Transform through the word," "Honor the gods and past glory," "Give form to chaos"

#### Playable Archetypes

* Architect of Order, tasked with building a bridge where no one dares
* Forum orator, whose rhetoric threatens an entire noble faction
* Former gladiator, become keeper of the imperial tombs
* Wandering philosopher, in search of the remnants of true law

#### Internal Tensions and Fault Lines

* **Nobility vs. new wealth**: the old aristocratic lineages against citizens enriched through trade or craft
* **Faith of Pulbium vs. Seasonal Druidism**: the imperial religion is official, but in working-class quarters the nature cult is practiced
* **Republic or imperial nostalgia**: some wish to restore the monarchy; others defend the senatorial institutions
* **Art as power vs. art as desertion**: artists and philosophers are central, but also often in exile

#### Symbols, Proverbs, Objects

* **Symbol**: the Incised Sun, image of eternity and illumination
* **Proverb**: "Only imperfection deserves the attention of the gods."
* **Sacred object**: the Staff of the Lupercal, carried during rites of purification and fertility

### Lysandrians

*Children of the sea, masters of the gentle deception*

#### Origin and Cultural Profile

Descended from a confederation of maritime city-states, the Lysandrians have built their power on navigation, trade, and the art of persuasion. Their culture **values mobility, diplomacy, and nautical supremacy**, and is strongly urbanized. Lysandrian trade routes touch nearly every port in Marethar, and their merchants are known for knowing what they want before they ask for it.

#### Identity Tags

"To sail is to decide," "Everything has a price," "He who listens, commands"

#### Playable Archetypes

* Cartographer of the Harbor Guild, in possession of a lost map
* Naval contractor, accused of double-dealing with the city's enemies
* Orphan raised among the docks, risen to ambassador
* Mystic of the Faith of Thalassos, condemned for betraying the sacred route

#### Internal Tensions and Fault Lines

* **Merchant oligarchy vs. assembly democracy**: some ports want more political representation
* **Faith of Thalassos vs. Cult of Nautilos**: the same sea has two deities, and two ways of facing it
* **Sister cities in commercial war**: the fiercest conflicts are internal
* **Open trade vs. strategic secrecy**: sharing routes means exposing oneself

#### Symbols, Proverbs, Objects

* **Symbol**: the Spiral of Currents, emblem of change and adaptation
* **Proverb**: "He who cannot lie, cannot sail."
* **Sacred object**: the Inverted Astrolabe, used in rites to divert storms

### Orethians

*Where stone sings of war and justice*

#### Origin and Cultural Profile

The Orethians are a mountain civilization, born around mining, metallurgy, and military strategy. They **value discipline, martial honor, and the continuity of blood and word**. Cities are carved into rock and defended by layered fortresses. Warrior families coexist with castes of artisans and miners in a rigorous balance.

#### Identity Tags

"Guard with strength," "Swear by iron," "Become stone"

#### Playable Archetypes

* Captain of the Black Steel, ready to betray for an inheritance
* Artisan of the Sacred Forge, in search of lost metal
* Militant orator of the Faith of Tudeshkhast
* Blind strategist, who sees beyond the mountain

#### Internal Tensions and Fault Lines

* **Ritual war vs. total war**: divergences on how and when to fight
* **Cult of war vs. cult of memory**: Tudeshkhast and Shemshahva offer opposing visions
* **Rigid hierarchy vs. military meritocracy**: traditionalist clans clash with young generals
* **Dwindling resources**: the wealth of the mines grows ever less certain

#### Symbols, Proverbs, Objects

* **Symbol**: the Broken Hammer, icon of implacable justice
* **Proverb**: "He who leaves the rock has no more name."
* **Sacred object**: the Oath Mask, worn only for binding vows

### Thalassians

*Between the waves, the pact is law*

#### Origin and Cultural Profile

Descended from coastal and island peoples, the Thalassians live at the heart of currents and maritime trade. Their culture is founded on **practical collaboration, port efficiency, and the protection of the collective**, with a strong devotion to Thalaran, the Spirit of the Sea. Thalassian ports are open but carefully watched, and cities organized as networks rather than centers.

#### Identity Tags

"Act together, swim together," "The sea does not forgive the ego," "Every sail has a duty"

#### Playable Archetypes

* Harbor Master, accused of ritual corruption
* Deep-sea navigator who refuses every written route
* Boat captain devoted to Carthara, ready for anything for the cargo
* Protector of a coastal community threatened by a commercial alliance

#### Internal Tensions and Fault Lines

* **Free trade vs. protection of routes**: what to share, and with whom?
* **Faith of Thalaran vs. Cult of Carthara**: spiritual protection or material prosperity?
* **Islanders vs. port-dwellers**: divergences between those who live on the sea and those who manage its economy
* **Ecological fragility**: fish stocks and tides are changing, and the sea does not forget

#### Symbols, Proverbs, Objects

* **Symbol**: the Tidal Net, representation of the relationships between naval clans
* **Proverb**: "He who does not close the cargo, closes his own coffin."
* **Sacred object**: the Blessed Salt Plate, carried in the holds as a talisman

### Naharim

*The river is time that speaks*

#### Origin and Cultural Profile

A river culture par excellence, the Naharim developed along the great waterways, building an advanced and deeply spiritual agricultural civilization. **Their relationship with nature is symbiotic**, grounded in the cyclical celebration of floods, harvests, and purifications. Their social life is communal, egalitarian, and guided by priests and elder women.

#### Identity Tags

"Follow the rhythm of the water," "Cultivate together," "Offer without waiting"

#### Playable Archetypes

* Keeper of the Floods, who has never seen the river stop
* Visionary ceramicist, whose works influence the village's dreams
* Rural matriarch in struggle against a diverted canal
* Priest of the God Dor, put on trial for peaceful heresy

#### Internal Tensions and Fault Lines

* **Dor vs. Naiad**: the River God against the Queen of Springs
* **Oral tradition vs. religious codification**: some prayers must never be written
* **Faith and hydraulics**: who decides where the water must flow?
* **Community vs. urban individualism**: tension between villages and growing city-states

#### Symbols, Proverbs, Objects

* **Symbol**: the Delta Spiral, sacred form of fertile distribution
* **Proverb**: "The first sip belongs to the river."
* **Sacred object**: the Cup of the Inner Tides, used in rites of spiritual initiation
