## 🝇 Living Geography

*Everything that does not move, but forces you to move*

> *"Marethar is made not only of waters and lands. It is made of borders that lie, and of territories that remember."*
> — Opening table of the Topographic Codex of Fahya

### Marethar

*A calm, salted womb… until it chooses otherwise.*

> **Close your eyes at dusk over Marethar:**
> The wind carries the smell of brine and the distant cry of gulls.
> Waves lap the hulls with an ancient rhythm.
> The dying sun tinges the water with gold and crimson.
> You hear the whisper of the depths guarding forgotten secrets.

Marethar is an **inner sea**, vast and seemingly calm, but do not trust the surface. Its waters, deep and rich with mineral salts, are studded with **fortified islands, submerged reefs, and sunken ruins**. Navigation is easy, for those who possess the right maps. Storms, though rare, **arrive with omens and vanish without leaving prisoners**.

Its waters connect every shore of the continent, making it a **commercial, spiritual, and military artery**. But the sea is not neutral: every nation attempts to control its stretches, headlands, and invisible straits. Whoever controls a port controls a season.

* **Resources**: fish, shellfish, seaweed, routes
* **Tensions**: piracy, fishing rights, coastal fortifications
* **Play**: naval blockades, mysterious disappearances, storms driven by ancient forces

> Some nautical charts mark islands that no longer exist, or that exist only at certain tides.

### The Mountains

*The silent guardians, and often sharp*

#### The Western Mountains

> **Close your eyes among the peaks of the Western Mountains:**
> The air is thin, scented with resin and wet stone.
> Silence is broken only by the rustle of wind through rock.
> Every step resonates like the echo of ancient stories.
> The cold stings the skin, but the heart warms at the thought of local legends.

They separate the [[Empire of Doria]] from the [[Republic of Premia]]. Seen from a distance, a wall. Up close, a **vertical labyrinth of forgotten paths and abandoned mines**. Rich in gold, iron, and other metals, they are also the refuge of bandits, hermits, and peoples who have chosen to answer to no king.

* **Resources**: gold, iron, coal
* **Tensions**: border disputes, mining rights, access to water sources
* **Play**: underground exploration, shifting frontiers, mining feuds

#### The Rashian Mountains

> **Close your eyes at dawn on the Rashian slopes:**
> The air stings the nostrils with the acrid smell of living stone.
> The sound of the pickaxe is rhythmic, but not human.
> Your breath tastes of copper and cold dust.
> And when the wind shifts, it carries a carved prayer: unspoken.

To the northwest, cradle of the [[Rashian Empire]], they are colder and higher. The mines here **never sleep**: gems, rare powders, and alchemical alloys are extracted. Mountain rituals scrape the rock; priests tattoo the granite. Those born here walk with hardened feet and a sharpened tongue.

* **Resources**: precious stones, rare metals
* **Tensions**: traffic in forbidden minerals, military control, environmental tensions
* **Play**: economic sabotage, maps of forbidden tunnels, hypogean cults

### Sacred and Living Forests

*They do not grow. They listen.*

> **Close your eyes among the trunks of Mirath:**
> The humidity clings to the skin like a regret.
> The moss smells of sweet ferment and wet bark.
> Every sound is close, but nothing shows itself.
> Beneath your feet, the earth is never still: it pulses, it remembers.

The forests that border Marethar, from [[Diarchy of Ashania|Ashania]] to the inner [[Empire of Doria|Dorian]] valleys, are never merely vegetation. **Every grove has a secret name and a voice**: the trees speak through accumulation, through inclination, through sustained silences. Some canopies respond to rituals. Others ignore those not born in the neighboring villages.

In [[Diarchy of Ashania|Mirath]], trunks are branded with druidic symbols and family genealogies. In [[Aridonians|Aridonia]], there are trees that bow only once a year, to indicate where the last of the singers is buried. In [[Empire of Doria|Doria]], some forest paths appear only in the fog, and lead to temples that no one built.

* **Resources**: ritual timber, rare herbs, psychotropic fungi, uncodified secrets
* **Tensions**: rights of passage, conflicts between druids and engineers, recurring disappearances
* **Play**: living maps, shifting groves, trees that bring dreams, or amnesia

### Plains and Hills

*Where peace is sown and war is reaped*

#### The Plains of Vallan

> **Close your eyes in the Plains of Vallan:**
> The scent of ripe grain mingles with that of fertile earth.
> Birdsong accompanies the rustle of stalks in the wind.
> The sun caresses the skin with reassuring warmth.
> You feel life pulsing beneath every clod, a promise of abundant harvests.

The granary of Marethar. Here the **earth is dark and full**, broken only by **irrigation canals like veins on fertile skin**. Security is an illusion: whoever controls the plains controls the bread. And bread is worth more than gold.

* **Resources**: grain, vegetables, rural labor
* **Tensions**: taxation, agrarian revolts, river manipulation
* **Play**: disputes between lordships, ritual burnings, peasant prophecies

#### The Hills of Ashania

> **Close your eyes among the vineyards of Ashania:**
> The aroma of fermenting must spreads through the air.
> Cicadas sing in chorus beneath the scorching sun.
> The limestone soil crunches beneath your feet.
> Every sip of wine tells stories of families and centuries-old traditions.

Gentle only for those who do not have to climb them on foot. Famous for their **tart wines and lunar olives**, but also for the **silent duels between rival noble families**. Behind every vineyard, an ancient vendetta.

* **Resources**: wine, oil, rare aromatics
* **Tensions**: boundaries between estates, rhetoric and customary law
* **Play**: agricultural scandals, harvest sabotages, secret alliances among vintners

### Sworn Mountains

*Every peak bears a name no longer spoken. Every gorge, an oath never revoked.*

> **Close your eyes in the forge of Natzrel:**
> The metal sings before it is struck.
> The air is thick, redolent of coal, promise, and pride.
> The sweat is not human: it is part of the ritual.
> And even the silence weighs, like an unspoken oath.

The mountains of Marethar **are not merely natural barriers**: they are **coffers of binding memory**. Unlike the high [[Rashian Empire|Rashian]] crests dominated by imperial mines, these heights rise in the heart of [[Kingdom of Vallan|Vallan]], where every stone bears a name and every path carries memory. The [[Kingdom of Vallan|Vallanese]] regard every peak as a sentinel and every valley as a tribunal. In the border gorges, **feuds are passed down like religious vocations**. Weapons forged in the highland forges are blessed multiple times: at the start, at the finish, and at the first killing.

Some mines are still in use. Others have been sealed *not for exhaustion*, but because it is said they "found something that was not stone." Paths change slowly, but when they do, it is as though they are *deciding*.

The highland sanctuaries are silent. And watched.

* **Resources**: iron, tin, black copper, ritual stone
* **Tensions**: border oaths, armed houses, disputed paths
* **Play**: weapons that speak, monk-forgers, sanctuaries that refuse those without Vallanese blood

### The Islands

*Refuges, frontiers, and floating lies*

> **Close your eyes beneath the walls of Allasia:**
> The wind tastes of salted copper and unsigned promises.
> Gulls cry like weary sentinels.
> The air vibrates between arches and towers, as if awaiting a verdict.
> And every wave that strikes the foundations seems to ask: "Have you returned, or are you new?"

[[City-State of Allasia|Allasia]] is **fortress, university, and port**. The nearby islands serve those who can afford them: military bases, exiled sanctuaries, commercial dens. Nothing is neutral along these shores.

* **Resources**: ports, knowledge, strategic position
* **Tensions**: wars of influence, diplomatic sabotage, naval inheritances
* **Play**: maritime espionage, secret academies, forbidden trade

---

### The Coasts

*Every port is a promise. Every island, a debt.*

> **Close your eyes in an Allasian cove at dawn:**
> The smell is brine and oil.
> The wind speaks too many languages.
> The waves arrive weary, like one who has too much to say.
> And the sea, today, pretends to sleep.

The [[Thalassians|Thalassian]] coasts that surround Marethar are **less linear than the map suggests**. Jagged, studded with ports that feign neutrality and coves that change name with every captain, they are **moving theaters**: diplomacy, war, superstition, and commerce converge with every tide.

Maritime cities, like [[City-State of Allasia|Allasia]] and the ports of the [[Rhithian League]], **do not live on the sea: they live of the sea**. Routes are not merely commercial: they are oaths. Shallows are avoided more by superstition than by navigation. Some lighthouses light only if the captain is expected. Some reefs *choose* what to destroy.

Beneath the surface, ancient wrecks hold objects no one claims. And sometimes they give them back.

* **Resources**: routes, information, salt, fish, ancient debts
* **Tensions**: legalized piracy, contested naval concessions, cult of the tides
* **Play**: perilous crossings, ports sealed by feuds, messages left among the reefs

### Grasslands and Marshes

*Where the water commands, and the grass listens*

#### The Grasslands of Dajilia

> **Close your eyes in the Grasslands of Dajilia:**
> The wind carries the smell of tall grass and wildflowers.
> The distant gallop of horses blends with the beating of your heart.
> The infinite sky embraces the earth in a sacred silence.
> Every breath is a hymn to freedom and vastness.

Oceans of grass, migrating herds, mounted tribes that **leave no tracks, but leave laws**. Here a word given is worth as much as a treaty carved in stone. And it costs more.

* **Resources**: livestock, wool, leather
* **Tensions**: grazing routes, oral disputes between clans
* **Play**: ritual duels, sacred races, negotiations between nomadic chiefs

#### The Marshes of Meshdad

> **Close your eyes in the Marshes of Meshdad:**
> The air is thick with humidity and the scent of aquatic plants.
> Insects sing hypnotic melodies.
> The ground gives way beneath your feet, revealing hidden waters.
> Every sound is a riddle; every shadow a possible threat.

Soft and damp as the belly of a tired god. The marshes feed, but also **conceal and poison**. Every village has a rule that cannot be explained. And everyone claims not to be afraid.

* **Resources**: rare plants, medicinal herbs, fish
* **Tensions**: overlapping authorities, armed land reclamation
* **Play**: rites of passage, beings that imitate voices, maps that change

### Ritual Deserts and Shifting Sands

*They do not separate. They protect what does not wish to be found.*

> **Close your eyes in the midday Desert of Qadmath:**
> The light stings. The air smells of overheated stone and dry skin.
> Silence makes noise, but it is never the same twice.
> When you breathe, you taste sand between your teeth.
> And sometimes… it seems the wind is asking you a question.

The deserts of Marethar are not territories of transit: they are **living borders** that bend, withdraw, and take revenge. The most notable is the **Desert of Qadmath**, considered an initiatory zone for mages and ascetics: its dunes change direction, some caves vanish and return in different places, and certain wells "listen."

Further to the southeast spread the **Sands of Suleiman** (see also Unmapped Zones), whose song, it is said, reveals hidden guilt. The most experienced travelers stop every hour, even without reason, to "reassure the ground." Scorpions leave symbols. Caravans use no maps: they use handed-down phrases.

* **Resources**: salt, copper, nocturnal flowers, silences of high spiritual value
* **Tensions**: disputes between cults, ritual disappearances, mobile sanctuaries
* **Play**: trails that shift, riddles bound to the wind, initiatory trials where one must forget in order to pass

### Vertical Cities and Labyrinths of Power

*The places where the upper floors do not listen to the lower ones. But control them.*

> **Close your eyes in the corridors of Marnorum:**
> The walls taste of polished stone and burned wax.
> Silence is geometric. If a word falls, it is an error of symmetry.
> The air is filtered, codified, rewritten.
> And everything carries the flavor of something already approved.

Not all the cities of Marethar spread out horizontally. Some **grow in levels, grids, concentric rings, and non-communicating floors**. [[Empire of Linia|Marnorum]], the [[Empire of Linia|Linian]] capital, is a perfect example: the high streets never touch the low ones. Decisions are made at the top, but are felt only as decree.

In Fahya, the city **grows inward into the mountain**: the magical universities are carved into craters, protected by stone gates that open only with words now forgotten. No plant grows on the surface, but the entire city is a nursery of formulae and control.

These places are more than spaces: they are **ideologies made stone**. Every staircase is a border. Every window, a hierarchy.

* **Resources**: archives, occluded knowledge, urban control, invisible prisons
* **Tensions**: vertical insurrections, struggles between levels, access denied to "civic" spaces
* **Play**: escape from forbidden floors, three-dimensional maps, floors where time flows differently

### Unmapped Zones and Contested Areas

*Mentioning them is dangerous. Naming them, worse still.*

> **Close your eyes near the White Springs:**
> The sound of gushing water is pure and crystalline.
> The air is cool, with a scent of moss and wet stone.
> The landscape shifts imperceptibly, escaping comprehension.
> Every step is an incursion into the unknown; every breath, a discovery.

* **The White Springs**: said to be the true origin of the Dorian River. No one has ever seen them twice in the same place.
* **The Desert of Suleiman**: to the southeast, the sand sings. Sometimes it asks back for what was never given to it.
* **The Island That Moves**: it appears in commercial maps from three centuries ago, then in stories for children, then, they say, in the dreams of certain captains.

**Contested areas:**

* **The [[Rashian Empire|Rashian]]-[[Republic of Premia|Premian]] Ridge**: every official map bends it to its own advantage. In reality, **the families there live under two laws and three moral codes**.
* **The Meshdad Delta**: fishermen, alchemists, and smugglers quarrel over the same stretch of water. Often at night.
