## 🝣 The Great Nations

*Frontiers, prides, and deep-rooted tensions*

> *"Every nation believes itself the only true voice. But history, like the earth, is made of overlapping strata."*
> — epigraph carved on a forgotten portal in Preiacoria

### The Republic of Premia

*The voice of law, the heart of reform*

Premia presents itself as **a republic of princes and senators**, an ordered and rational land where power is transmitted through election and consent. But beneath the marbles of the capital, the roots are older, more warlike. Born from a revolt against a decadent monarchy, Premia **has never ceased to fear tyranny**, and for this reason seeks to control everything, even itself.

> **Close your eyes in a field at the gates of Preiacoria:**
> The smell is of grain cut too early and parchment rewritten.
> Footsteps on the cobblestones make noise only for those who fear them.
> The air tastes of thin iron and waiting.
> You hear the votes: not words, but weights, falling one by one.

The Senate of Preiacoria governs the vast plains, the cultivated hills, and the sacred forests with an almost mathematical rigor. The republican legions are disciplined and used sparingly. The economy, mixed between agriculture, metals, and craftsmanship, is among the most solid.

But stability has a price: **the growing distance between patricians and commoners, between city and countryside, between center and periphery**. Internal tensions, so far contained, are beginning to generate cracks in the corridors of power.

Preiacoria is the institutional and symbolic heart of the republic: a labyrinth of colonnades and senate, where words are weighed more than decrees. To the north, Livia stretches between vineyards and academic gardens, famous for its civic academies where knowledge is debated in meter and philosophical disputes outlast elections. Further south, Aurelinum guards a precarious balance between rurality and tradition, offering asylum to minor cults and schools of customary law that silently challenge republican orthodoxy.

* **Contradictions**: Formal equality and actual inequality; cultural reformism and social conservatism
* **External pressures**: Economic rivalry with Meshdad, political suspicions from Hamania
* **Mini hook**: A consul mysteriously disappears before the vote on a new agrarian law. The Senate calls for discretion, but the square already cries "treason."

### The Diarchy of Ashania

*Two crowns, one uncertain people*

Ashania is an elegant and contradictory land, **governed by a diarchy that alternates harmony with strategic frost**. King Athelstan and Queen Elara reign together… but rarely from the same hall. Their dualism, celebrated in seasonal rites, reflects an unstable balance between rural nobility and urban classes, between tradition and pragmatism.

The Diarchy lives off herbalism, timber, and light mining, but also off **well-woven alliances**. Its forests are dense, sacred, and dangerous; its valleys produce light wines and heavier tensions. The Royal Guard is small but tenacious, sustained by local militias tied to lordships and noble houses.

> **Close your eyes among the trunks of Mirath:**
> The humidity clings to the skin like a regret.
> The moss smells of sweet ferment and wet bark.
> Every sound is close, but nothing shows itself.
> Beneath your feet, the earth is never still: it pulses, it remembers.

Ashania has built its reputation on diplomacy, but its daily reality is made of **silent negotiations and unwritten pacts**. And beneath the roots of the forests of Mirath, some say things grow that kings have never dared name.

Durtus, ceremonial center of the Diarchy, alternates seasons of silence and celebration like the very rhythm of governance. Beneath the twisted branches of the sacred forests, Mirath grows slowly around potions and pacts: a laboratory for healers, druids, and diplomats who speak little but cut deep. In Elaranor, nestled among the wooded hills, the minor noble houses exchange favors in public and poisons in private.

* **Contradictions**: Shared sovereignty and personal ambitions; commercial openness and ancestral distrust
* **External pressures**: Linian expansionism, protection requests from border villages
* **Mini hook**: An ancient stele found in the Elara Mountains shows the faces of three sovereigns. But Ashania has always had only two crowns. Always?

### The Empire of Linia

*The order that does not ask permission*

Linia **does not define itself as a nation. It defines itself as the measure of what is just.** Its emperor, Valerian III, governs with an iron fist and impeccable rhetoric. Every citizen knows what they must do. Every road knows where it leads. Every imperial wine knows when it must be poured.

The capital, Marnorum, is a colossus of marble and metal. The plains and mountains surrounding it are rich in grain, minerals, and above all **codified rhetoric**. The economy is broad and diversified, founded on intensive agriculture, commerce, and civil engineering.

> **Close your eyes in the corridors of Marnorum:**
> The walls taste of polished stone and burned wax.
> Silence is geometric. If a word falls, it is an error of symmetry.
> The air is filtered, codified, rewritten.
> And everything carries the flavor of something already approved.

But order is not merely infrastructure: it is imposition. The Senate, consultative, serves more to reflect the emperor's will than to balance it. **Linia's legions are the most numerous and best equipped in Marethar**, ready to defend or "normalize" whatever disturbs the symmetry.

Internally, the Empire struggles to contain noble rivalries, the growing influence of magical colleges, and a people that **is beginning to doubt infallibility as the foundation of the State**.

Marnorum is the heart of the Empire: marble, symmetry, and living archives where laws correct themselves before they are even promulgated. Corvalis, less visible but no less central, is a city of training and rhetorical architecture, where orators equip themselves with words as others equip themselves with blades. Lutria, stretched along the river, is less rigid but more dangerous: a crossroads of cultural influences and a port of ideas balanced between heresy and innovation.

* **Contradictions**: Propaganda of perfection and invisible corruption; urban magnificence and rural imbalances
* **External pressures**: Diplomatic clashes with Meshdad, customs friction with Premia
* **Mini hook**: A minor region of the empire spontaneously elects a "People's Prefect" inspired by Premian models. The Emperor orders him ignored. But three cities have already joined him.

### The Republic of Meshdad

*The palace is built of spice and sand*

Those who arrive in Meshdad for the first time see a city of perfumes. But those who live there know that the dominant smell is not that of spices: it is calculation.

Meshdad is a sultanate with a mercantile soul and a heart divided between tent and palace. Governed by Sultan Farid II, flanked by a council of viziers and governors, the city spreads like a market: modular, expansive, rich in facades and back rooms.
The culture is Aridonian, but the spirit is that of one who always has something to sell, or to buy.

> **Close your eyes at the center of the covered bazaar:**
> There are too many spices. Every smell contradicts the last.
> The voices are high, then low, then gone.
> The fabrics brush against you, but seem to appraise you.
> And the sweat tastes of metal and waiting.

Trade in spices, metals, and textiles has made Meshdad **a power without legions but with currencies capable of bending alliances**. Its army, modest, is formed of mercenaries and militias, more skilled at defending the borders of profit than waving banners.
Internal tensions, however, grow: **the desert nobility and the bazaar bourgeoisie have not spoken to each other for decades**, except through delegates and diplomatic stilettos.

Meshdad has no center: it has centers, each with its own market, its own prophet, its own debt. Among its alleys, Tarikia changes face every day: at first light it is a spice bazaar, at dusk a theater, at night a confessional. Zarhabil, further away, lives like a high-born widow: withdrawn, elegant, always ready to offer comfort to those who pay in coins or secrets.

* **Contradictions**: Aristocracy and commerce in silent war; urban splendor and water crisis in the desert
* **External pressures**: Hostility with Dajilia, friction with Rashia, dependence on maritime routes
* **Mini hook**: A new variety of spice, capable of inducing lucid dreams, appears in the markets. No one knows where it comes from. But many are ready to kill to control it.

### The Grand Duchy of Hamania

*Where every grain of sand bears an engraved oath*

Hamania presents itself with the face of Grand Duke Arslan III, but **speaks with the voice of a thousand satraps**. It is a vast and hard land, made of deserts and mountains, but also of **sprawling cities and nomadic clans with long memory and short tongue**.

Power is shared only formally: in reality every satrapy is a semi-nation, with its own alliances, personal armies, and codes of conduct never set to parchment.
Resources, metals, salt, spices, make Hamania **rich and jealous**, but its greatest wealth is the pride of its chiefs. And pride, in Marethar, always costs blood.

> **Close your eyes in a sandy courtyard:**
> The sun enters your nostrils before the sand does.
> The air smells of leather, salt, and forgotten iron.
> The echoes are more numerous than those present.
> And someone, always, watches you in silence.

The grand duchy has a large and well-armed army, alternating siege tactics with wars of honor. But the true battles are fought **in the halls of tribal accords, where a proverb can shatter an alliance more than a hundred muskets**.

Busgalerey, the "nomadic" capital, stretches and dissolves like a parchment in the wind: today a court, tomorrow a market, the day after an open-air tribunal. Karnak is quarry and house, a city stratified vertically where clans descend deeper the more they lose prestige. Darhan has no walls, but those who cross it know that every tent has eyes, and every fire a memory to burn or protect.

* **Contradictions**: Formal monarchy and functional anarchy; centralism and clan rivalries
* **External pressures**: Hostility with Meshdad, Rashia, and Dajilia; uneasy alliance with Rhithia
* **Mini hook**: During an ancestral ceremony, an ancient tribal seal is stolen. Without it, an entire satrapy loses legitimacy, and peace.

### The City-State of Allasia

*Where the sea has voice, and the land pays forfeit*

Allasia does not consider itself a city. It considers itself **a route**. A sacred intersection where the sea, commerce, and intelligence shake hands, with a sword behind their backs.

> **Close your eyes in the inner harbor of Allasia:**
> The smell is brine and oil.
> Footsteps on the wood creak only if you have something to hide.
> The wind speaks too many languages.
> And the sea, today, pretends to sleep.

Governed by a doge elected from the most influential families, and by a council of merchants and nobles, Allasia is **a maritime democracy for those who can afford it**, and a gilded prison for those who serve at its docks.
The culture is Thalassian, the language slides like oiled silk, and power runs through the fleet.

Its ships, military and commercial, control **routes, embargoes, and noisy diplomatic negotiations**. The city prospers on fish, salt, pearls, and everything that can be bought or pretended to have been stolen.

But the markets have become more ruthless than the seas: **the families fight for routes, ports, and cargo shares as in a war without declared weapons**.

Allasia has no face: it has one for every family that governs it. In the harbor, sails speak more than councilors; in the inner palaces, auctions are held in silence, conducted only with nods and contracts of glances. Beyond the main canal stretches Noctir, where smuggling masquerades as art, and art as law. Toward the hill, Virelia houses maps that no longer exist and cartographic tales worth more than a fleet.

* **Contradictions**: Appearance of harmony and continuous mercantile feuds; freedom of speech and absolute economic control
* **External pressures**: Hostility with Dajilia, complex alliances with Rhithia and Linia
* **Mini hook**: A ghost ship docks by night. No one saw it arrive. Its cargo is composed only of sealed crates… and a single word carved on each side: "Return."

### The Rashian Empire

*When the sword shapes the law*

Rashia is **more a rhythm than a kingdom**. It is the sound of armor on the plains, the ceremonial drums that precede the proclamation of a new governor, the wind that runs over the fortified palaces of Fahya. Governed by Khalid IV, the empire **does not ask for consent: it takes what it considers order**.

Once a mosaic of warrior tribes, today Rashia is **an organized and impatient state machine**, measuring value in territories and dinars. Its economy is founded on precious metals, spices, and salt, and its military strength is among the most feared: heavy artillery, standing armies, large-scale siege tactics.

> **Close your eyes at the foot of the walls of Fahya:**
> The stone tastes of stored heat and warning.
> The smell of iron comes from within.
> The voices cannot be heard, but can be sensed.
> And every sound seems to say: "Stay in your place."

But the heart of the Empire **is as unstable as its glory**: struggling nobles, fractious provinces, and a population that alternates veneration with fear. Every external success risks igniting a new internal front.

Fahya is fortress, university, and imperial showcase: every stone has a plaque, every library an armed guardian. To the south, Al-Qamar breathes the desert with menacing elegance: it is said that here astrologers speak to the Emperor every night. Khaldun, nestled among the mountains, is the mother of legions: its quarries yield not only stone, but sculpted strategy.

* **Contradictions**: Forced unification and persistent tribal identities; central authority and feudal autonomies
* **External pressures**: Open hostility with Meshdad, Hamania, and Dajilia; strategic alliance with Doria
* **Mini hook**: An imperial caravan disappears in the Desert of Qadmath. The Emperor orders silence. But someone begins to receive letters signed by those who were aboard.

### The Kingdom of Vallan

*Forge and blood, mountain and oath*

Vallan is a kingdom of another age, and claims it with pride. King Thane I sits on a throne forged at Natzrel with black iron and ancient oaths, while his legions train in the gorges and mines.
Every village has a smith. Every temple, a sacred weapon.

> **Close your eyes in the forge of Natzrel:**
> The metal sings before it is struck.
> The air is thick, redolent of coal, promise, and pride.
> The sweat is not human: it is part of the ritual.
> And even the silence weighs, like an unspoken oath.

Vallanese identity is founded on **discipline, honor, and ancestral memory**. Resources are abundant: minerals, timber, rare herbs. And so are the feuds. The kingdom is a mosaic of armed houses, united only so long as the king proves he deserves the weapon he carries.

Militarily, Vallan is powerful: mountain training, heavy armor, and knowledge of the terrain. But the greatest threat is not external: it is **the temptation of personal power, always lurking behind every shield**.

Natzrel is the forged capital: a ring of black stone and tempered wood where every street bears the name of a vendetta. Khazar extends along the contested forests, and houses armed markets where every pact is engraved in iron. Vorr, atop the most ancient passes, is a city of oaths: here promises are exchanged before witnesses… living or otherwise.

* **Contradictions**: Cult of unity and endemic clan rivalries; sanctity of the crown and tendency toward ritual rebellion
* **External pressures**: Latent hostility with Linia, commercial tensions with Rashia
* **Mini hook**: A new league of three minor houses proposes electing a "king of the borders." It is merely an honorary title… until it is not.

### The Empire of Doria

*The river remembers what the books deny*

Doria has no need of armies to assert itself. It has the River Dor. And the river **never stops**.

The Dorian Empire is an intricate network of river cities, ceremonial palaces, and granaries full of grain. Its strength is agricultural, logistical, ritual. Water is sacred; every irrigation is an act of power; every dam, an edict.
The population, numerous and tenacious, follows the flood cycles like divine calendars.

The empress governs with a lethal calm, like the current that seems gentle but drags away every obstacle. The economy is based on irrigated agriculture, fishing, and river craftsmanship. Its legions fight little, but control every bridge, every ford, every landing.

> **Close your eyes along the Dor at Vera:**
> The river does not flow. It passes.
> It tastes of silt, agricultural sacrifice, and prayers never recorded.
> The boats move without sound.
> And every drop is a witness.

But the calm is apparent. **Peasants and nobles no longer trust one another**, and late rains are always someone else's fault.

Dor, which gives the river its name and takes its rhythm, reclines on river terraces like a wet parchment. Horus is a marsh-city, half submerged and completely listened to: it is said its waters record everything. Nilat, city of migrant seeds, lives with floods and famines as sisters: blessed when they alternate, feared when they ally.

* **Contradictions**: Water centralism and social fractures; sanctity of balance and thirst for autonomy
* **External pressures**: Mutual distrust with Premia, strategic alliance with Rashia
* **Mini hook**: A new current in the river carries unknown seeds. The harvests change. Some bloom in winter. And the temple falls silent.

### The Principality of Dajilia

*Between ridges and oaths, an impossible balance*

Dajilia is a knot, not a line. A crossroads of paths, migrant pastures, wandering clans, and vows spoken to the wind. Governed by Aram II, a prince as respected as he is isolated, the principality lives in **the constant attempt to mediate between honor and memory**.

Founded around the springs of Nabad, the capital is a ring of stone and wood that protects its waters with more words than walls. Every tribe has the right to cross its borders, or to be excluded from them for seven generations. Resources are few but contested: wool, basalt, salt, medicinal herbs. Water is not abundant, but is free only to those who have sworn their name on the right stone.

Militarily, Dajilia is light and silent: fast cavalry, highland archers, mobile patrols along vegetated borders. But the principality's true weapon is **shared memory**: every name, every vow, every betrayal is engraved. And the stones, on the plateaus, remember on their own.

> **Close your eyes before a spring at Nabad:**
> The water tastes of promises.
> The wind carries voices that no one dares forget.
> The tall grass bends, but does not break.
> Every drop knows your name, even before you speak it.

Nabad is the highland capital, a circle around the water where every spring is guarded by law, song, and threat. Alak rises among rocks and shepherd-worn paths: there they read the stars, but also the maps drawn by those who know how to lose their way with dignity. Rakeb, say the clans, moves: today a waystation, tomorrow a refuge, the day after a city, but only for those who find it twice without asking.

* **Contradictions**: Tribal solidarity and dynastic rivalries; central governance and inviolable oral justice
* **External pressures**: Tensions with Meshdad, Rashia, and Hamania
* **Mini hook**: A dry crater fills with water in the dead of night. No one dug it. Those who look into it dream the same lion of shadow and fire.

### The Rhithian League

*When an island casts a shadow longer than the continent, it is not the sun that deceives: it is the fleet.*

The Rhithian League **is not an empire, but behaves as if it were**. A constellation of city-states, each with its own accent, its own sails, and its own ambitions, that gave itself a single name in order to speak louder. The pulsing heart is Lurocedina, but the blood flows through every port, bay, and stopover where a blue-gold banner with the six-pointed star flies.

> **Close your eyes listening to the market of Lysandria at dawn:**
> The auctions are whispered between the nets.
> Every cry is a plausible lie.
> The wine barrels have a false bottom.
> And the fishermen… fish only by day.

Led by a council of archons and dominated by the most influential merchants of the Rhithian islands, the League proclaims itself united against external threats, but within the walls of its arsenals, **the city-states challenge each other daily for control of routes, innovations, and new discoveries**.

The culture is Lysandrian: it celebrates navigation as art, discovery as virtue, and commerce as a well-rigged gamble. The festivals are oceanic, the naval races spectacular, and every new nautical map is a treasure that can shatter an alliance.

**The League's fleet is its body, but its mind is cartography.** No power knows Marethar better; none reads the winds as they do. But every discovery ignites appetites, and every contested port is an excuse to send "armed observation ships."

* **Contradictions**: Political unity as facade, violent rivalries between city-states; celebration of discovery and censorship of secret routes
* **External pressures**: Tensions with Dajilia over southeastern routes; tactical alliance with Hamania; mutual distrust with Meshdad
* **Mini hook**: An old nautical atlas reappears at Lurocedina. It contains routes that no longer exist, or that should not exist. Who brought it back? And why are some pages written in a language forgotten even by the Rhithians themselves?
