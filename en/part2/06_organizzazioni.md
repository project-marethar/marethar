## 🜗 Guilds, Orders, and Academies

*The networks that sustain power when crowns fall*

> *"He who commands today was kneeling before a master merchant yesterday."*
> — popular saying in Meshdad

In Marethar, **strength is never enough. Structure is needed. Organized memory is needed. Collective recognition is needed.**
Guilds, orders, and academies are not merely places of knowledge or work: they are **parallel political systems**, that dictate unwritten laws, protect families, and ruin dynasties.
Every nation has its palaces. But many of those palaces cannot stand without the timber, seals, or formulae provided by those who work in the basements of power.

---

### Merchant and Artisan Guilds

#### Merchants' Guild of Meshdad

*They speak with a low voice, but their scales weigh heavier than a cannon*

The Guild controls much of the trade in spices and textiles. It is not merely an economic consortium: it is **a mercantile diplomacy in the form of a council**, capable of stopping a war simply by relocating a trade fair.

Presided over by an "Arbiter of Flavor," elected every five years, it regulates prices, defines routes, and, when necessary, imposes internal sanctions.
**Every city with a recognized emporium is bound to the Guild's commercial charter.**

> *Influence*: Decisive in Meshdad and Hamania, growing in Premia and Rhithia
> *Rites*: The "Oath of Spices" is performed blindfolded, in a room where every scent has a precise meaning

---

#### Artisans' Brotherhood of Vallan

*Smiths, weavers, carpenters: masters, not servants*

Every Vallanese artisan belongs to a brotherhood. Every brotherhood has a code, a genealogical tree, and a vendetta pending or honored.
The Brotherhood protects its own, but demands **absolute rigor in quality and conduct**. Mastery tournaments are true diplomatic events.

* *Internal division*: every craft has its master craftsman, but internal conflicts are regulated by a Council of Hands
* *Occult powers*: some maintain that the Brotherhood guards "living" metals and forgotten knowledge of ritual forging

---

#### Naval Builders' Corporation of Lysandria

*The sea does not forgive imperfections*

This corporation **decides who may launch a ship, how, and with what blessing**. Without the seal of Lysandria, no ship enters the ports of Rhithia without risking embargo.

Every master carpenter is also a keeper of formulae, proportions, and oaths.
The balance between technique and superstition is absolute: **a single error in construction can mean shipwreck… or curse.**

> *Rites*: Keels are marked with numerical chants and symbolic dyes; every step is documented with inscriptions that only members can read

---

### Religious and Magical Orders

#### Order of Mages of Thalassia

*They do not practice magic: they practice invisible governance*

The mages of Thalassia **are not hermits: they are functionaries, counselors, protectors**. Organized in a council led by an Archmage, they exercise arcane and juridical power.
The Order offers protection against supernatural threats and counsel to ambassadors and city-states.

He who breaks a pact with a mage **does not merely break a contract: he breaks the balance with forces he does not know.**

* *They accept apprentices only through prophetic recommendations.*

---

#### Druidic Brotherhood of Dimilalica

*Nature is not a good to be venerated. It is a voice to be heard.*

The druids gather around the Arcilde, the keepers of the major cycles. They have no fixed seat, but every sacred forest is a living archive.
They deal with agriculture, ritual health, and also rural justice. Some refuse the use of writing; others codify everything in encoded verse.

* *Rites*: seasonal ceremonies are directed by at least three druids, beneath plants marked with animal blood and resin incense

---

#### The Black Hand

*If no one knows who commands, it is because everyone obeys*

It is not a guild. It is not a sect. It is **a shadow with fingers everywhere**, born in Meshdad and grown where official power looked away.

It manages smuggling, extortion, networks of informants, and political assassinations.
Every "finger" of the Hand acts independently, but obeys a single rule: **never be noticed twice in the same place.**

> *Feared by nobles and admired by merchants. No one dares speak of it. Everyone speaks of it.*

---

#### Brotherhood of Rashian Assassins

*The blade that seals secrets*

Recognized and feared throughout Marethar, the Rashian assassins do not act for money, but **to protect noble balances, dynasties, or arcane pacts**.

The Master of Shadow leads the Council of Twelve, each of whom guards a lethal technique and a secret name.

* *Every mission is a ceremony; every victim a silent testament.*

---

### Academies and Institutions of Knowledge

#### Magical University of Fahya

*Where even silence has its own grimoire*

A center of magical excellence, but also **an observatory of power and arcane orthodoxy**, the university welcomes students from across Marethar.
Divided into specialized towers, the university guards **alchemical secrets, registries of forbidden formulae, and archives that even the professors cannot consult**.

* *The examinations are oracular. Some students do not even pass the first dream.*

---

#### College of Philosophers of Preiacoria

*Reason is the most vulnerable temple*

The college trains **jurists, diplomats, astronomers, but also poet-assassins and masters of noble rhetoric**.
Open in structure, but hierarchical in debate: those who cannot defend a thesis cannot write it.

Many Premian senators come from here.

* *Some sections of the college are interdicted during certain equinoxes. No one explains why.*

---

#### Academy of Nabad

*Anyone can learn. But not everyone can stay.*

In the heart of Dajilia, the Academy is **a crossroads of knowledge and training**. It trains strategists, artists, healers, and weapons masters.
The rector and council decide who may advance to the next level, but **ritual duels outweigh examinations**.

* *Some say that an entire discipline is taught only in dreams, during the season of the winds.*
