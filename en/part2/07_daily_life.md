## 🝟 Daily Life

*What happens when heroes sleep and the markets reopen*

> *"Politics moves armies, but it is hunger that moves hands."*
> — marginal annotation in the Diary of Simple Days, Preiacoria

### Food: Inhabiting Taste

In Marethar, **food is geography**. Every region has a flavor; every nation, a distinct table. Eating is a social, cultural, spiritual act, at times ritual. Some communities bless the meal before serving it. Others **keep its ingredients silent** out of respect for the seasons or the guests.

* **In [[Aridonians|Aridonia]]**, strong spices and dried meats are consumed, with scented water and flatbread. The meal is often silent, followed by shared stories only when the moon is full.
* **In [[Lysandrians|Lysandria]]**, grilled fish and fresh salads mark port life: quick, light meals, shared between citizens who never stop debating.
* **In [[Orethians|Orethia]]**, one eats to endure: thick soups, spiced roasts, aged cheeses, and strong ales, always accompanied by war stories or mine songs.
* **In [[Naharim]]**, meals follow the rhythm of the river: freshwater fish, rice, legumes, and vegetables accompany every season with sweets of milk and fruit.

Food is not merely consumed: **it is offered, exchanged, feared**. Some tables carry blessings. Others, secrets.

### Clothing and Fashion: The Form of Function

Dressing in Marethar is **survival, status, and symbol**. Fabrics speak of climate, culture, and power. The stitching, at times, is worth as much as a ring.

* **[[Thalassians|Thalassia]]** wears linen and silk, with maritime embroidery and vivid colors: port practicality, but with a taste for ceremony.
* **[[Aridonians|Aridonia]]** dresses in coarse cotton and turbans, with natural fabrics that protect from sun and sand.
* **[[Orethians|Orethia]]** reinforces itself with wool and leather, adding light plates even to civilian dress: war is always a hypothesis.
* **[[Naharim]]** favors fine cotton, brilliant colors, and jewelry in gold and lapis lazuli: riverine elegance, almost mystical.

> *"A man without a belt has no word."*
> — Aurelian saying on the value of ceremonial buckles

### Seasonal Rhythms and Festivals: The Year as Prayer

Every culture in Marethar **reads the year as a story**. Some divide it into four chapters; others into thirteen cantos. The seasons are not merely changes of weather: they are **occasions for telling, honoring, giving thanks, or fearing**.

* **[[Thalassians|Thalassia]]** celebrates the sea with the **Festival of Waves** and the **Merchants' Day**, amid regattas and banquets.
* **[[Aridonians|Aridonia]]** gathers around the fire for the **Desert Solstice** and dances in the rain during the **Water Rites**.
* **[[Orethians|Orethia]]** honors the fallen in the **Mining Festivals**, with offerings of iron and ale to the ancestors.
* **[[Naharim]]** celebrates the flood and the dry season with **nocturnal songs and purification rites** in the sacred waters.

Some days are **interdicted**: work is done in silence, mirrors are covered, certain names are avoided. Others are **pure spectacle**: fires, ceremonial races, masked processions.

### Family, Bonds, Social Relations

The concept of family varies:

* In **[[Naharim]]**, it is **extensive and spiritual**: one who eats at the same table for seven days is a "water-sister."
* In **[[Orethians|Orethia]]**, **family discipline is military**: every father is also a trainer.
* In **[[Aridonians|Aridonia]]**, blood counts, but **the oath is worth more than lineage**: one may change house, but never dishonor an engraved oath.
* In **[[Lysandrians|Lysandria]]**, families are **more ideological than biological**: they form around workshops, assemblies, voting circles.

Social relations follow **invisible grids**: who may speak first, who may sit at the head of the table, who pours the wine. But every rule has its exceptions. And every transgression, its consequences.

---

### Work and Leisure

Leisure exists, but **is never neutral**. It is dance, game, reflection, or public performance.

* The **[[Orethians]]** compete in archery or endurance contests.
* The **[[Lysandrians]]** love street theater and public rhetorical games.
* The **[[Thalassians]]** tell stories in taverns, accompanied by marine sounds.
* The **[[Empire of Doria|Dorians]]** practice a form of river chess, with colored wooden pieces and rules that change with the tide.

> In some cities, **resting is considered a sign of nobility.** In others, it is punished with social oblivion.
