# Aridonians

*Where life is resistance and memory is sand*

## Origin and Cultural Profile

The Aridonians descend from nomadic tribes settled in the deserts and highlands of southern Marethar. Though today they also live in well-structured oasis cities, they **maintain a strong bond with ancestral practices**, in particular the cult of ancestors, clan honor, and the rhythms of the desert.

They are known for their **resilience, tribal structure, and deep communal sense**. Their architecture is mobile or semi-permanent, cities are founded around water sources, and life is measured by solar cycles, ritual migrations, and collective celebrations.

## Identity Tags

"Honor the ancestors," "Survive together," "Never forget an offense"

## Playable Archetypes

* Daughter of the Sand, ready to take her mother's place as keeper of stories
* Caravan warrior, seeking redemption after breaking an oath
* Former priest of Shahin, in exile after a heretical dream
* Young chieftain, fighting against the order of the assembled chiefs

## Internal Tensions and Fault Lines

* **Tradition vs. innovation**: Some oases open to outside trade; others wish to maintain ritual purity.
* **Cult of Shahin vs. Golat Tradition**: the Sect of Shahin is dominant, but in border zones the shamanic cult of the Yellow Panther gains followers.
* **Role of women**: respected everywhere, but in some tribes they hold full political power; in others, only symbolic.
* **Clanism vs. federation**: the Council of Chiefs seeks unity, but the oldest clans want autonomy.

## Symbols, Proverbs, Objects

* **Symbol**: the Flayed Tree, emblem of the life-death-regeneration cycle
* **Proverb**: "He who forgets his grandfather's name loses his shadow."
* **Sacred object**: the Clay Lion, simulacrum of courage used in the Sand Ceremonies

## Extended Profile

**Culture Name:** Aridonian

**Basic Information:**

* **Origins:** The Aridonians descend from ancient nomadic tribes that inhabited the plains and deserts of Marethar. These tribes evolved into more structured and sophisticated societies over the centuries, yet retained many of their ancestral traditions.
* **Primary Language:** Aridonian
* **Total Population:** 14,401,227

**Cultural Information:**

* **Core Traditions:** Aridonian traditions are deeply rooted in nomadic life and desert survival practices. The Summer Solstice celebration, during which the tribes gather to feast with dancing, song, and endurance contests, is one of the year's most important events. Aridonian culture places great emphasis on honor, family, and community.
* **Festivals and Celebrations:** Beyond the Summer Solstice, Aridonians celebrate the Year of the Lion, a festival held every twelve years to honor strength and courage, symbolized by the desert lion. The Sand Ceremony is another important ritual, in which young adults prove their maturity through a series of physical and mental trials.
* **Social Structure:** Aridonian society is organized into clans and tribes, each led by an elected tribal chief. The council of tribal chiefs meets regularly to make decisions at regional level. Aridonian women command great respect and frequently occupy positions of power within the tribes.
* **Art and Architecture:** Aridonian art is known for its intricate metalwork and jewelry, often set with precious stones and tribal motifs. Architecture is characterized by adobe structures and tents decorated with vivid textiles. Oasis-cities serve as cultural and commercial centers.

**Religious Information:**

* **Primary Religion:**
  * **Religion Name:** Sect of Shahin
  * **Supreme Deity:** Shahin, God of the Tree
  * **Description:** The Sect of Shahin venerates Shahin, a deity representing life, growth, and protection. The religion is characterized by a strong communal spirit and mystico-religious practices that emphasize harmony with nature and ancestral spirits.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 5,909,228
* **Minor Religions:**
  * **Religion Name:** Golat Tradition
  * **Supreme Deity:** Golat, the Yellow Panther
  * **Description:** The Golat Tradition centers on shamanism, with Golat as its supreme deity. The faithful perform shamanic rituals to commune with spirits and obtain protection and wisdom.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 1,445,255

**Technological Information:**

* **General Technology Level:** Medium
* **Key Technologies:** Advanced metallurgy and bronze-working techniques, hydraulic engineering for oasis irrigation, and sophisticated firearms and cannons.
* **Notable Innovations:** The Aridonians are known for their advanced irrigation systems that enable agriculture in the desert, and for their techniques of metal extraction and working, particularly bronze and silver.
