# Principality of Dajilia

*Between ridges and oaths, an impossible balance*

Dajilia is a knot, not a line. A crossroads of paths, migrant pastures, wandering clans, and vows spoken to the wind. Governed by Aram II, a prince as respected as he is isolated, the principality lives in **the constant attempt to mediate between honor and memory**.

Founded around the springs of Nabad, the capital is a ring of stone and wood that protects its waters with more words than walls. Every tribe has the right to cross its borders, or to be excluded from them for seven generations. Resources are few but contested: wool, basalt, salt, medicinal herbs. Water is not abundant, but is free only to those who have sworn their name on the right stone.

Militarily, Dajilia is light and silent: fast cavalry, highland archers, mobile patrols along vegetated borders. But the principality's true weapon is **shared memory**: every name, every vow, every betrayal is engraved. And the stones, on the plateaus, remember on their own.

> **Close your eyes before a spring at Nabad:**
> The water tastes of promises.
> The wind carries voices that no one dares forget.
> The tall grass bends, but does not break.
> Every drop knows your name, even before you speak it.

Nabad is the highland capital, a circle around the water where every spring is guarded by law, song, and threat. Alak rises among rocks and shepherd-worn paths: there they read the stars, but also the maps drawn by those who know how to lose their way with dignity. Rakeb, say the clans, moves: today a waystation, tomorrow a refuge, the day after a city, but only for those who find it twice without asking.

* **Contradictions**: Tribal solidarity and dynastic rivalries; central governance and inviolable oral justice
* **External pressures**: Tensions with Meshdad, Rashia, and Hamania
* **Mini hook**: A dry crater fills with water in the dead of night. No one dug it. Those who look into it dream the same lion of shadow and fire.

## Detailed Data

**Political System:**

* **Government Type:** Principality
* **Capital:** Nabad
* **Current Leader:** Prince Aram II
* **Government Structure:** Hereditary monarchy with a council of elders advising the prince

**Territories:**

* **Total Area (km²):** 734,784
* **Principal Regions:** Heights of Nabad, Plateaus of Alak, Valleys of the Winds
* **Natural Borders:** Rocky ridgelines, seasonal wetlands, bands of vegetative shadow

**Dominant Culture:**

* **Culture Name:** Aridonians of the Grasslands
* **Traditions and Customs:** Semi-nomadic life, ritual caravans, solar and votive celebrations, deep reverence for sworn oaths
* **Official Language:** Aridonian

**Resources and Economy:**

* **Principal Natural Resources:** Copper, rock salt, ritual herbs
* **Principal Industries:** Light mining, copper-working, transhumant herding, caravan trade
* **Trade and Exchange:** Active with central and eastern territories; ritual caravans keep ancestral routes alive
* **Currency:** Dajilian Dinar
* **General Economic Description:** A mobile but structured economy, founded on control of the passes and the ritualization of trade routes. Agriculture is limited but supported by highland irrigation techniques.

**Military Forces:**

* **Army Size:** Moderate
* **Military Structure:** Light troops, fast cavalry, mountain archers
* **Technologies and Armaments:** Light firearms, composite bows, flexible armor
* **Naval Fleet:** Negligible; river fleet and caravan escorts
* **Military Strategies:** Mobile skirmishing, control of passes, symbolic patrols

**Additional Information:**

* **International Relations:** Neutral with Rhithia, historical wariness toward Hamania, latent tensions with Rashia and Meshdad
* **Internal Issues:** Inter-clan friction, tensions over broken oaths, control of water sources
* **Brief History:** It arose as a confederation of clans who fled from the south, preserving the ritual form of the caravan, and consolidated as a Principality with its capital in the highlands. Every alliance is founded on words spoken and remembered.
