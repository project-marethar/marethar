## 🝆 Lost Objects, Relics, Artifacts

*Some powers have no voice. Only form.*

> *"A king without a crown can still command. But a people without relics has already lost the future."*
> — An Aridonian proverb

The objects that Marethar preserves, or forgets, are never merely what they appear to be. **Some are magical artifacts. Others, symbols. Others still, lies that became truth**. They are often lost. Other times they are hidden, denied, or transformed into political, religious, or philosophical instruments.

### The Crown of Shahin

**Origin**: Aridonia
**Function**: Symbol of spiritual guidance and oath of peace
**Rumours**: It is said that whoever wears it dreams of future wars every night, but cannot prevent them. Some tribes believe the crown speaks, but only to those who have lost everything.
**Status**: Kept at the Sanctuary of Shahin, though some maintain the current crown is a replica.

### The Sword of Ser Athelstan

**Origin**: Ashania
**Function**: Sacred weapon, gifted by the druids of Dimilalica
**Rumours**: It cannot strike the same enemy twice; if it does, the blow falls on the bearer instead. Some insist the sword carries a fragment of arboreal spirit.

### The Flame of Ralertis

**Origin**: Linia
**Function**: A torch that never goes out, used in processions, purifications, and oaths
**Rumours**: It illuminates only the truth, but blinds whoever lies while holding it. Some philosophers argue the torch emits not light, but memory.
**Status**: Missing for over two centuries; its last mention appears in a diplomatic treaty burned halfway through.

### The Mirror of Carthara

**Origin**: Thalassia
**Function**: Divinatory instrument; reflects not appearance, but intent
**Rumours**: Used to adjudicate contracts between captains. Broken once, and only at sea. Some say gazing into it too long causes one to forget one's own name.
**Status**: Currently in the possession of a naval corporation that uses it as a moral seal.

### The Amulet of Shahin

**Origin**: Aridonia
**Function**: Protective talisman, often worn during rites of passage or desert journeys
**Rumours**: It protects only those who have never betrayed. Some say it ceases to function if the bearer kills in cold blood.

### The Wanderer's Compass

**Origin**: Dajilia
**Function**: It does not point north: it points toward what the heart desires most.
**Rumours**: They say whoever follows it without hesitation always arrives… but not where they expected. Some use it to test the truth of their own longing.
**Status**: Appeared in at least seven markets, each time with a new owner and a new legend.

### The Book of the Second Life

**Origin**: Fahya
**Function**: Ritual tome. Said to contain the spell for bringing exactly one person back from the dead.
**Rumours**: No one knows the cost. Some say it brings back only the body; others say it steals life from whoever reads it. It has been sealed with red wax, and the wax does not melt.
**Status**: Held in a forbidden library. No one knows which one.

### The Lost Imperial Seal

**Origin**: Aurelians
**Function**: Allows the bearer to command those who still swear loyalty to the imperial line.
**Rumours**: It awakens dormant memories, compels fealty, but erases every other allegiance. Some maintain that whoever wears it can rewrite history, one word at a time.
**Status**: The object of contention in at least three military campaigns. No one has displayed it in the past fifty years.

### How to Use Them in Play

These objects are not merely **tools with bonuses**. They are **narrative catalysts**.

> **Narrative use ideas**:

* Objects that activate only under emotional, moral, or historical conditions
* Relics that change form or effect depending on the bearer's culture
* "Useless" objects that are nonetheless central to a vision, a dream, or a revelation
* False artifacts, created to manipulate entire communities
