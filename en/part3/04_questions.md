## 🜓 Three Questions Without Answers

*Some questions have no solutions. But they have echoes.*

> *"Truth in Marethar is like wind through cloth: you feel it, but no one can grasp it."*
> — Fragment carved on a broken bell in Fahya

### 1. What Is Shahin, Truly?

**Context:**
Venerated by the Aridonians as a sacred tree, a spirit, a mother-or-father deity. Some cults call it a god. Others say it is **a natural process**, a collective consciousness generated by ritual, or **the memory of a species**, rooted like sacred vegetation.

**Current hypotheses:**

* Shahin is real, but has never had a face: **it is every oath kept**, and manifests in those who do not betray.
* Shahin is an invention forged to unite the clans, created by a druidic council in a remote age.
* Shahin **is dead**, and what is venerated today is only the ritual echo of a vanished being.

**Game seeds:**

* A druid claims to have found the roots of Shahin… in a Thalassian marsh.
* A young person receives dreams from Shahin that contradict every known dogma.
* A new sect proclaims that Shahin has returned, but under a new name, and with new precepts.

### 2. Where Do the Creatures of Marethar Come From?

**Context:**
Sirens, ifrits, griffins, golems, rainbow serpents… Creatures spoken of everywhere, yet **no one can document their existence with certainty**. And yet, their power in culture and history is undeniable.

**Current hypotheses:**

* They are real, but **bound to ages or altered states of consciousness**: they appear only when the world is "off-balance."
* They are collective projections of ancient fears or memories, **generated by faith and oral transmission**.
* They are **"sentient aeons"**: creatures that live outside time and use Marethar as a temporary vessel.

**Game seeds:**

* A recovered library contains a treatise with a biological classification of ifrits. Some names match recent witnesses.
* A mythic creature appears at a village… and asks to be forgotten.
* A cultist claims he can "generate" a legendary creature, if enough people believe in its existence.

### 3. What Was the True Origin of the Empire of Doria?

**Context:**
According to official texts, the river Empire of Doria arose along the Dor through a unified cult and the guidance of an enlightened dynasty. But oral sources, apocryphal texts, and censored chronicles **tell irreconcilable versions**.

**Current hypotheses:**

* The river **existed before the cities**, and it was its spirit that chose the first rulers.
* The Empire is **a successful magical experiment**, an alchemical creation in state form, sustained by a rite still active today.
* Doria **never existed as a unified empire**: it is a narrative imposed by Fahya academics to justify a fluvial dominion.

**Game seeds:**

* An ancient Dorian seal is discovered in territory that appears on no imperial map.
* The river's water begins to "reject" certain official rituals.
* A new doctrine proclaims that the Empire is dead… but that the river has begun to choose a new people.
