## 🜚 How Do You Enter the World?

*Starting a campaign, a chronicle, a narrative drift*

> *"Marethar never begins in the same place. Sometimes it's a deal, sometimes a corpse, sometimes a map no one ever drew."*

Every entry into Marethar is a declaration. There are no tutorials, no neutral openings. What you choose to see first — and who you choose to be when you see it — determines the direction in which the world will bend.
Here are four **entry structures**, each suited to long campaigns or self-contained sessions, each capable of showing Marethar in a different light.

### Diplomatic or Political Campaign

*Nations in tension, agreements, treaties, betrayals*

**Where you play:** between embassies, senates, courts, strategic ports, contested routes.
**Who you portray:** advisors, nobles, spies, officers, clergy with indirect power.
**Opening questions:**

* Who truly wants war... and who is only pretending to?
* What is the agreement that cannot be officially signed?
* What is really being exchanged under diplomatic banners?

**Suggested tones:** elegant conspiracy, ritual language, civil masks that slip.

### Localized Micro-Fiction

*A village, a ship, a monastery, a broken road*

**Where you play:** isolated, peripheral, or self-governing places.
**Who you portray:** locals, wanderers, guardians, newcomers.
**Opening questions:**

* What is here that no one wants discovered?
* Who is the one person who cannot leave?
* What does everyone expect to happen, even if no one says it?

**Suggested tones:** slow mystery, suspended time, broken cycles.

### 🕯 Esoteric Investigation

*A cult, an object, a creature that may not exist*

**Where you play:** locked libraries, sealed ruins, cities that lie.
**Who you portray:** scholars, heretics, sorcerers, witnesses, victims.
**Opening questions:**

* Why is this thing being sought now? Who first spoke of it?
* What was concealed — and by whom?
* What happens if the truth is actually found?

**Suggested tones:** metaphysical horror, moral ambiguity, faith that fractures.

### Memorial Exploration

*Ruins, legends, dreams that do not align with history*

**Where you play:** submerged cities, destroyed archives, deserts threaded with song.
**Who you portray:** archaeologists, former priests, last survivors, failed translators.
**Opening questions:**

* Which event do people remember too differently?
* What presence should no longer be there?
* Who swore never to speak of it... but is now ready to talk?

**Suggested tones:** elegy, fate, self-discovery through what has been erased.
