## 🜎 Tensions as Plot

*No adventure need be written: just let a fault line pull you in*

> *"Tension is not the problem. It is what happens when no one can avoid it any longer."*

In Marethar, history does not move in a straight line. It spreads like cracks in old walls: it begins where no one is watching, but eventually passes through everything.
Every campaign grows from an **unresolved tension**. A fault line waiting for no more than a breath, a lie, a misplaced choice to split open.

Here are five **thematic axes**, each ready to generate stories, factions, conflicts, and dilemmas.

### Politics

*Who commands. Who is excluded. Who rewrites the laws.*

Politics in Marethar is **layered, ritual, but never stable**. Every title conceals a debt. Every reform, a vendetta. Every alliance, a future rupture.

**Questions that generate stories:**

* Who has just acquired a power they do not know how to use?
* Which law applies, but no one dares name aloud?
* Which minority faction needs only one lie to become a majority?

**Putting it in play:**

* Introduce a treaty no one wants to sign, but everyone pretends to have read.
* Have an unexpected figure elected.
* Have war declared without anyone knowing against whom.

### Religion

*Heresies, apparitions, divine silences.*

Faiths coexist with skepticism, but **no one lives without a rite**. The gods speak rarely. And sometimes they speak to those who should not be listening.

**Questions that generate stories:**

* Which cult speaks a truth that cannot be accepted?
* Who has had a vision... and who believes them?
* What happens when a sacred rite fails in public?

**Putting it in play:**

* A sacred relic is denounced as a forgery.
* A new prophet speaks words everyone recognizes, but from the wrong mouth.
* A temple stops working. But it continues to receive offerings.

### Family

*Lineages, contested inheritances, honors to be redeemed.*

In Marethar, **every family is a map of debts and expectations**. Names carry weight. Marriages are wars. Children never choose entirely for themselves.

**Questions that generate stories:**

* Who changed their name... and why?
* Who inherits a title they never wanted?
* Who must avenge something they do not remember?

**Putting it in play:**

* Produce a will bearing unrecognized signatures.
* Introduce an illegitimate heir everyone prefers.
* Reveal a blood tie in a place where lying would serve better.

### Knowledge

*Censorship, rewriting, secrets revealed too soon.*

Knowledge is a fragile power. Those who hold it defend it with fire or silence. **Every library is also a prison.**

**Questions that generate stories:**

* Which text must not be read?
* Who lied while writing a chronicle?
* Who discovered something... and immediately forgot it?

**Putting it in play:**

* A manuscript contradicts the entire official history of a city.
* An arcane formula is revealed... and everyone begins dreaming the same dream.
* An oracle is made public. But it changes every time it is quoted.

### Territory

*Ritual disputes, wrong maps, places that shift.*

The lands of Marethar **are never merely geography**. They are alliances, scars, untold histories. Some valleys remember. Some rivers choose which way to flow.

**Questions that generate stories:**

* Who lives in a place others consider sacred?
* Which border was drawn... but never accepted?
* What happens when a city vanishes from the map?

**Putting it in play:**

* A neutral zone is claimed by two different cults.
* The ground itself shifts: it sinks, it rises, a bridge disappears.
* An official map shows a place that has never existed. Until today.
