## 🜄 The Cultures of Marethar

### Aridonia

**Culture Name:** Aridonian

**Basic Information:**

* **Origins:** The Aridonians descend from ancient nomadic tribes that inhabited the plains and deserts of Marethar. These tribes evolved into more structured and sophisticated societies over the centuries, yet retained many of their ancestral traditions.
* **Primary Language:** Aridonian
* **Total Population:** 14,401,227

**Cultural Information:**

* **Core Traditions:** Aridonian traditions are deeply rooted in nomadic life and desert survival practices. The Summer Solstice celebration, during which the tribes gather to feast with dancing, song, and endurance contests, is one of the year's most important events. Aridonian culture places great emphasis on honor, family, and community.
* **Festivals and Celebrations:** Beyond the Summer Solstice, Aridonians celebrate the Year of the Lion, a festival held every twelve years to honor strength and courage, symbolized by the desert lion. The Sand Ceremony is another important ritual, in which young adults prove their maturity through a series of physical and mental trials.
* **Social Structure:** Aridonian society is organized into clans and tribes, each led by an elected tribal chief. The council of tribal chiefs meets regularly to make decisions at regional level. Aridonian women command great respect and frequently occupy positions of power within the tribes.
* **Art and Architecture:** Aridonian art is known for its intricate metalwork and jewelry, often set with precious stones and tribal motifs. Architecture is characterized by adobe structures and tents decorated with vivid textiles. Oasis-cities serve as cultural and commercial centers.

**Religious Information:**

* **Primary Religion:**
  * **Religion Name:** Sect of Shahin
  * **Supreme Deity:** Shahin, God of the Tree
  * **Description:** The Sect of Shahin venerates Shahin, a deity representing life, growth, and protection. The religion is characterized by a strong communal spirit and mystico-religious practices that emphasize harmony with nature and ancestral spirits.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 5,909,228
* **Minor Religions:**
  * **Religion Name:** Golat Tradition
  * **Supreme Deity:** Golat, the Yellow Panther
  * **Description:** The Golat Tradition centers on shamanism, with Golat as its supreme deity. The faithful perform shamanic rituals to commune with spirits and obtain protection and wisdom.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 1,445,255

**Technological Information:**

* **General Technology Level:** Medium
* **Key Technologies:** Advanced metallurgy and bronze-working techniques, hydraulic engineering for oasis irrigation, and sophisticated firearms and cannons.
* **Notable Innovations:** The Aridonians are known for their advanced irrigation systems that enable agriculture in the desert, and for their techniques of metal extraction and working, particularly bronze and silver.

### Aurelia

**Culture Name:** Aurelian

**Basic Information:**

* **Origins:** The Aurelians descend from an ancient empire that dominated the central region of Marethar. Over time they absorbed cultural influences from neighboring peoples, creating a rich and complex civilization.
* **Primary Language:** Aurelian
* **Total Population:** 3,095,627

**Cultural Information:**

* **Core Traditions:** Aurelian culture is strongly shaped by imperial heritage. Celebrations of military victories, public games, and festivals honoring the gods are common. Lantern Week, an event celebrating art and science with light displays and public lectures, is a much-anticipated annual occasion.
* **Festivals and Celebrations:** Religious festivals such as the Dies Natalis (birthday of the principal gods) and the Lupercalia (feast of purification and fertility) are celebrated with great fervor. The Carnival of Eternal Glory is an annual event honoring Aurelian culture and tradition with parades, theatrical performances, and banquets.
* **Social Structure:** Aurelian society is stratified, with a clear division between the nobility, common citizens, and slaves. Social mobility is possible, however, through military service and the accumulation of wealth. Women of noble rank can wield considerable political and cultural influence.
* **Art and Architecture:** Aurelian art is known for its frescoes, mosaics, and statues, often depicting mythological and historical scenes. Architecture combines classical elements with recent innovations such as monumental arches, domes, and amphitheaters.

**Religious Information:**

* **Primary Religion:**
  * **Religion Name:** Faith of Pulbium
  * **Supreme Deity:** Pulbium, the Ancient
  * **Description:** The Faith of Pulbium venerates the Ancient as a symbol of wisdom and protection. Shamanic rituals and ancestor veneration are integral to religious practice.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 24,860
* **Minor Religions:**
  * **Religion Name:** Druids of Dimilalica
  * **Supreme Deity:** Dimilalica, the Summer Jaguar
  * **Description:** This religion celebrates nature and seasonal cycles, with rituals honoring the sun and the earth. The Druids of Dimilalica practice nature worship through sacred ceremonies and prayers.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 2,330

**Technological Information:**

* **General Technology Level:** High
* **Key Technologies:** Advanced engineering, monumental architecture, sophisticated agricultural and irrigation techniques, and refined firearms and artillery.
* **Notable Innovations:** The Aurelians are known for the construction of roads, aqueducts, and amphitheaters, as well as advanced techniques in the production of weapons and armor.

### Lysandria

**Culture Name:** Lysandrian

**Basic Information:**

* **Origins:** The Lysandrians derive from ancient city-states that flourished along the coasts of Marethar. Their history is rich in maritime trade and exploration, which brought them to become one of the most influential cultures in the region.
* **Primary Language:** Lysandric
* **Total Population:** 3,020,137

**Cultural Information:**

* **Core Traditions:** Lysandrian traditions revolve around trade, navigation, and exploration. Naval competitions, sporting games, and celebrations of discovery are common events. The culture prizes the art of seamanship and commercial acuity.
* **Festivals and Celebrations:** The Festival of the Sea is the most important celebration, where city-states compete in naval races and feast with banquets and fireworks. Merchant's Day honors trading traditions with open markets and cultural exchange.
* **Social Structure:** Lysandrian society comprises a dominant merchant class, free citizens, and slaves. City-states are governed by councils of elders or merchant oligarchies. Women participate actively in trade and may hold prominent roles in the city-states.
* **Art and Architecture:** Lysandrian art is known for its elaborate ship carvings, marine frescoes, and mosaics. Architecture is characterized by buildings with ornate facades and well-planned harbors. City-states are renowned for their magnificent temples and lively markets.

**Religious Information:**

* **Primary Religion:**
  * **Religion Name:** Faith of Lysandria
  * **Supreme Deity:** Thalassos, God of the Sea
  * **Description:** The Faith of Lysandria venerates Thalassos, God of the Sea, regarded as protector of sailors and merchants. Rituals include offerings of fish and precious objects cast into the sea to secure divine protection during voyages.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 2,000,000
* **Minor Religions:**
  * **Religion Name:** Cult of Nautilos
  * **Supreme Deity:** Nautilos, Lord of the Waves
  * **Description:** The Cult of Nautilos is a minor religion that worships Nautilos, Lord of the Waves. The faithful believe Nautilos controls the tides and can bring either prosperity or destruction. Rituals include prayers and ritual chants performed at the sea's edge.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 1,020,137

**Technological Information:**

* **General Technology Level:** High
* **Key Technologies:** Advanced shipbuilding techniques, navigation instruments, maritime architecture, and sea-trade methods.
* **Notable Innovations:** The Lysandrians are known for the construction of fast and durable ships, the development of detailed nautical charts, and the creation of advanced lighthouses to guide vessels into harbor.

### Naharim

**Culture Name:** Naharim

**Basic Information:**

* **Origins:** The Naharim are a river culture that prospered along the great rivers of Marethar. Their civilization developed around intensive irrigation and agriculture, becoming one of the most important centers in the region.
* **Primary Language:** Naharim
* **Total Population:** 5,158,319

**Cultural Information:**

* **Core Traditions:** Naharim traditions are closely bound to the river's cycle and to agriculture. Harvest celebrations and festivals honoring the rivers are central to their culture. Naharim society places great emphasis on communal cooperation and respect for nature.
* **Festivals and Celebrations:** The River Festival, which marks the beginning of flood season, is the year's greatest celebration. The community gathers to give thanks to the river for its abundance and to make offerings to the river gods. The Harvest Festival is another important celebration, featuring dancing, singing, and banquets.
* **Social Structure:** Naharim society is organized into villages and city-states governed by elders and priests. The social structure is egalitarian, with a strong sense of community and cooperation.

  Women play a central role in managing agricultural resources and in spiritual life.

* **Art and Architecture:** Naharim art is characterized by intricate ceramics, richly decorated textiles, and murals depicting scenes of daily and mythological life. Architecture includes monumental temples, bridges, and canals ingeniously designed for irrigation.

**Religious Information:**

* **Primary Religion:**
  * **Religion Name:** Cult of Dor
  * **Supreme Deity:** Dor, God of the River
  * **Description:** The Cult of Dor venerates Dor, God of the River, regarded as the source of life and prosperity for Naharim civilization. Rituals include offerings of fruit, flowers, and works of art cast into the river to secure divine favor.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 4,000,000
* **Minor Religions:**
  * **Religion Name:** Faith of the Water Spirits
  * **Supreme Deity:** Naiad, Queen of the Waters
  * **Description:** The Faith of the Water Spirits worships Naiad, Queen of the Waters, and a host of minor spirits inhabiting rivers and springs. Rituals include purification ceremonies and prayers for the protection of the waters.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 1,158,319

**Technological Information:**

* **General Technology Level:** Medium
* **Key Technologies:** Advanced irrigation techniques, river engineering, intensive agriculture, and construction of temples and hydraulic infrastructure.
* **Notable Innovations:** The Naharim are known for their systems of canals and dams that control flooding and improve agricultural productivity. They have also developed advanced techniques for food preservation and intensive cultivation.

### Orethia

**Culture Name:** Orethian

**Basic Information:**

* **Origins:** The Orethians derive from ancient mountain peoples who inhabited the elevated regions of Marethar. Their culture evolved around mining and weapons production, making them one of the most militarized civilizations in the region.
* **Primary Language:** Orethian
* **Total Population:** 1,888,322

**Cultural Information:**

* **Core Traditions:** Orethian traditions are closely bound to mountain life and war. Military initiation ceremonies and festivals honoring war gods are central events. Orethian culture celebrates strength, courage, and loyalty.
* **Festivals and Celebrations:** The Festival of the Warrior, which honors military heroes and celebrates victories in battle, is one of the most important. Participants don ceremonial armor and take part in martial skill competitions. The Festival of the Forge is another key celebration, dedicated to craftsmen and their creations.
* **Social Structure:** Orethian society is strongly hierarchical, with a dominant military class and a rigid caste system. Tribal chiefs and warlords hold power, while craftsmen and miners are respected for their skills. Women may attain positions of command in both military and social domains.
* **Art and Architecture:** Orethian art is characterized by intricate metalwork, sculptures of heroes, and battle scenes. Architecture includes mountain fortresses, robust castles, and ingeniously constructed mines. Cities are often fortified and built in stone.

**Religious Information:**

* **Primary Religion:**
  * **Religion Name:** Faith of Tudeshkhast
  * **Supreme Deity:** Tudeshkhast, the Flying Sage of Destiny
  * **Description:** The Faith of Tudeshkhast is grounded in dualism and venerates Tudeshkhast as a symbol of balance between light and darkness, life and death. Rituals include offerings and prayers in dedicated temples situated on mountain summits.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 6,012,780
* **Minor Religions:**
  * **Religion Name:** Faith of Shemshahva
  * **Supreme Deity:** Shemshahva, the Brown Cobra
  * **Description:** The Faith of Shemshahva centers on ancestor veneration and the worship of Shemshahva. Rituals include offerings to ancestors and prayers for guidance and protection.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 1,372,268

**Technological Information:**

* **General Technology Level:** Medium-High
* **Key Technologies:** Advanced mining techniques, metalworking, military engineering, and fortification construction.
* **Notable Innovations:** The Orethians are known for the creation of high-quality weapons and armor, the use of advanced mining techniques, and the construction of complex mountain defense systems.

### Thalassia

**Culture Name:** Thalassian

**Basic Information:**

* **Origins:** The Thalassians derive from ancient coastal peoples who inhabited the islands and shores of Marethar. Their culture evolved around maritime trade, navigation, and fishing, making them one of the region's naval powers.
* **Primary Language:** Thalassian
* **Total Population:** 3,611,967

**Cultural Information:**

* **Core Traditions:** Thalassian traditions are deeply rooted in seafaring life and trade. Sailing competitions, festivals honoring sailors, and celebrations of great discoveries are central to their culture. Thalassian society values nautical skill, commercial ingenuity, and cooperation.
* **Festivals and Celebrations:** The Festival of the Waves is the most important celebration, where coastal communities gather to feast with sailing races, fish banquets, and fireworks. Trade Day honors merchant traditions with open markets and cultural exchange.
* **Social Structure:** Thalassian society comprises a dominant merchant class, sailors, fishermen, and craftsmen. City-states are governed by councils of merchants or naval oligarchies. Women may hold significant roles in trade and city-state administration.
* **Art and Architecture:** Thalassian art is known for its intricate ship carvings, marine frescoes, and mosaics. Architecture includes well-planned harbors, lighthouses, warehouses, and temples dedicated to sea deities. Coastal cities are characterized by colorful houses and lively markets.

**Religious Information:**

* **Primary Religion:**
  * **Religion Name:** Spirits of Thalaran
  * **Supreme Deity:** Thalaran, Spirit of the Sea
  * **Description:** The Spirits of Thalaran venerate Thalaran as protector of seas and waters. Rituals include offerings of fish and precious objects cast into the sea to secure protection during voyages.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 6,621,114
* **Minor Religions:**
  * **Religion Name:** Cult of Carthara
  * **Supreme Deity:** Carthara, Protector of Merchants
  * **Description:** The Cult of Carthara worships Carthara as the patron deity of traders and maritime travelers. The faithful offer sacrifices and prayers to secure prosperity and protection.
  * **Number of Faithful:** 1,630,629

**Technological Information:**

* **General Technology Level:** High
* **Key Technologies:** Advanced shipbuilding techniques, navigation instruments, harbor architecture, and sea-trade methods.
* **Notable Innovations:** The Thalassians are known for the construction of fast and durable ships, the development of detailed nautical charts, and the creation of advanced lighthouses to guide vessels into harbor.
