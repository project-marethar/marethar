## 🜨 Religions of Marethar

### Spirits of Thalaran

**Type:** Folk
**Form:** Animism
**Culture:** Thalassian
**Area:** Thalassia
**Faithful:** 6,621,114

**Description:** The Spirits of Thalaran represent one of the oldest religions of Thalassia. This faith is deeply rooted in the maritime and coastal traditions of the Thalassians, who venerate the spirits of nature and of seafaring ancestors. The Spirits of Thalaran are considered protectors of the sea, the winds, and the creatures of the deep. The faithful believe that every element of nature possesses a spirit, and that ancestors continue to influence the world of the living through these spirits.

**Origins and Religious Practices:** The religion's origins reach back to the first inhabitants of the Thalassian coasts, who sought protection and guidance to face the challenges of the sea. Rituals include offerings of fish and other seafood, ritual chants, and dances celebrating the natural forces. Ceremonies are often conducted on the beach or aboard small vessels at sunset, a moment considered sacred.

**Clergy Structure:** The religion has no rigid clerical structure. Community elders and ships' captains serve as spiritual guides, leading the rituals and transmitting oral traditions to new generations.

### Cult of Carthara

**Type:** Folk
**Form:** Animism
**Culture:** Thalassian
**Area:** Thalassia
**Faithful:** 1,630,629

**Description:** The Cult of Carthara is a religion practiced primarily by Thalassian merchants and sailors. Carthara is venerated as the protecting spirit of journeys and commerce, offering protection during sea crossings and guaranteeing prosperity in trade. The faithful of Carthara make sacrifices and offer prayers before every voyage to secure divine favor.

**Origins and Religious Practices:** The cult originates in the ancient merchant traditions of Thalassia, where traders sought divine protection for their dangerous voyages across the sea. Rituals include sacrifices of coins and precious objects, and prayers recited before departure. Some sailors wear amulets blessed in Carthara's name.

**Clergy Structure:** The Cult of Carthara has no organized clergy. The most experienced ships' captains and merchants often assume the role of spiritual guide, leading the rituals and prayers.

### Sect of Shahin

**Type:** Organized
**Form:** Heresy
**Culture:** Aridonian
**Area:** Aridonia
**Faithful:** 5,909,228

**Description:** The Sect of Shahin venerates Shahin, God of the Tree, a deity symbolizing life, growth, and protection. This religion, considered heretical with respect to Aridonia's dominant beliefs, has a strong communal and mystical character. The faithful believe that Shahin protects the forests and guarantees abundance and fertility to the land.

**Origins and Religious Practices:** The sect arose as a reaction against dominant religious practices, focusing on a deeper connection with nature. Rituals include tree-planting ceremonies, offerings of fruit and flowers, and collective meditation beneath the sacred trees. The most important ceremonies take place at the solstices and equinoxes.

**Clergy Structure:** The Sect of Shahin has an organized clergy of druids and priestesses who guide communities, administer rituals, and protect the sacred groves. The clergy is trained in herbalism and the tending of trees.

### Marethan Atheism

**Type:** N/A
**Form:** N/A
**Culture:** Various
**Area:** Marethar
**Faithful:** 22,991,162

**Description:** Marethan Atheism represents the absence of organized religious beliefs. The atheists of Marethar rely on reason, science, and philosophy to understand the world and their place in it. This outlook rejects the existence of deities or spirits, focusing instead on human potential and scientific understanding.

**Origins and Practices:** Atheism took hold in Marethar as a reaction to religious wars and abuses of power by religious institutions. Its adherents value education, critical thought, and scientific innovation. There are no associated religious rituals, but philosophical gatherings and public debates are common.

**Clergy Structure:** There is no clergy in atheism. Leaders are often philosophers, scientists, and thinkers who promote rationalism and progress.

### Gods of Ralertis

**Type:** Organized
**Form:** Polytheism
**Culture:** Aurelian
**Area:** Aurelia
**Faithful:** 1,367,240

**Description:** The religion of the Gods of Ralertis venerates Ralertis, the Sapphire Reaper, and a pantheon of lesser deities. The faithful participate in complex rituals and celebrations honoring these deities for prosperity and protection. The religion emphasizes harmony between the gods and human beings, with rites celebrating the cycles of nature and human life.

**Origins and Religious Practices:** This religion has its origins in the ancient Aurelian civilizations, which believed in a pantheon of deities responsible for various aspects of life and nature. Rituals include offerings of jewels, fruits, and animals, alongside elaborate ceremonies involving chants, dances, and recitations of ancient myths. The principal festivities take place at the seasonal transitions and significant astronomical events.

**Clergy Structure:** The religion is guided by a well-structured clergy of priests and priestesses who serve in the temples dedicated to Ralertis and the lesser deities. They are responsible for performing the rituals, managing the temples, and providing the faithful with religious education.
