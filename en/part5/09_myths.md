## 🜃 Myths and Legends

This chapter gathers the myths, legends, and folk tales that form the living core of belief and worldview across the cultures of Marethar. These stories pass from generation to generation, shaping the traditions and identities of peoples who carry them the way others carry weapons, as something that protects.

---

### Creation Myths

#### The Creation of Marethar

* **Origin:** A story shared among the coastal cultures of Marethar.
* **Account:** In the beginning, [[Spirits of Thalaran|Thalaran]], the Spirit of the Sea, waged war against the Leviathan, the primordial sea-beast. During the battle, the waves rose until they swallowed the surrounding lands, carving out the great inner sea. Thalaran prevailed, and the waters of Marethar grew calm and fertile. What was destruction became home.

#### The Birth of the First Tribes

* **Origin:** A myth shared among the Aridonian tribes.
* **Account:** [[Sect of Shahin|Shahin]] planted the seeds of life in the desert of [[Aridonians|Aridonia]]. From those seeds the first nomadic tribes were born. They learned to live in harmony with the desert and its resources. Shahin taught them the arts of survival and of war, and stood between them and the creatures that hunted in the deep sands.

#### The Genesis of the Orethian Mountains

* **Origin:** A myth of the Orethian peoples.
* **Account:** The mountains of [[Orethians|Orethia]] were shaped by the powerful hand of [[Faith of Tudeshkhast|Tudeshkhast]], the Flying Sage of Fate. He molded the peaks to give his people shelter and the raw wealth of the deep earth. The mines rich in precious minerals are held to be a divine gift, and the mountains themselves are venerated as sacred ground.

### Legends of Heroes and Monsters

#### Lysander and the Sea Serpent

* **Origin:** A legend of the [[Rhithian League]].
* **Account:** Lysander, the naval hero, faced the dreaded Sea Serpent that had terrorized the waters of Marethar. With the aid of Thalassos, the God of the Sea, Lysander defeated the creature and secured the safety of the trade routes. His victory is celebrated every year during the Sea Festival.

#### The Feat of Ser Athelstan

* **Origin:** An epic tale of [[Diarchy of Ashania|Ashania]].
* **Account:** Ser Athelstan, co-ruler of Ashania, stood alone against an army of invaders to defend his kingdom. Armed with his enchanted sword, the gift of [[Druidic Orders of Dimilalica|Dimilalica]], the Summer Jaguar, he drove back the invaders and became a symbol of courage and loyalty. His legend still draws young warriors of Ashania to the forge and the oath-stone.

#### Zarah's Battle Against the Fire Dragon

* **Origin:** A legend of the mountain tribes of [[Orethians|Orethia]].
* **Account:** Zarah the Intrepid descended into the caverns of the Orethian mountains to face the dreaded Fire Dragon. Through cunning and resolve, she brought the creature low, earning the respect and wonder of her people. Her deed is told aloud at the Festival of the Forge.

### Folk Tales and Folklore

#### Malik and the Magic Lamp

* **Origin:** A folk tale of [[Republic of Meshdad|Meshdad]].
* **Account:** Malik, a young merchant, found a magic lamp during one of his journeys. When he rubbed it, a genie appeared and offered him three wishes. With wisdom, Malik used the wishes to bring prosperity to his city and became one of the most respected merchants in Meshdad. The magic lamp endures as a symbol of hope and fortune.

#### The Tale of the Sirens of Thalassia

* **Origin:** A sea legend of [[Thalassians|Thalassia]].
* **Account:** The Sirens of Thalassia, beautiful and dangerous, drew sailors toward them with enchanted song. Once, a young fisherman named Einar resisted their call and discovered their secret: an ancient enchantment that bound them to the sea against their will. With the help of [[Spirits of Thalaran|Thalaran]], Einar broke the enchantment and freed the Sirens. They have not forgotten.

#### The Legend of the Winged Horse of Vallan

* **Origin:** A heroic tale of [[Kingdom of Vallan|Vallan]].
* **Account:** During a great drought, a winged horse appeared on the plains of Vallan. Guided by the Harvest Spirits, it brought the rains that saved the crops. Since then, the winged horse is venerated as a sign of hope and abundance, and its story is told during the Harvest Festival.

### Legendary Objects and Sacred Sites

#### The Enchanted Sword of Ser Athelstan

* **Origin:** A legend of [[Diarchy of Ashania|Ashania]].
* **Account:** The [[The Sword of Ser Athelstan|enchanted sword of Ser Athelstan]], given to him by [[Druidic Orders of Dimilalica|Dimilalica]], is a weapon of legend. It is said to have the power to repel any invader and to protect the kingdom of Ashania. It is kept in the Temple of [[Druidic Orders of Dimilalica|Dimilalica]], where it is venerated as a symbol of divine protection.

#### The Lamp of Malik

* **Origin:** A tale of [[Republic of Meshdad|Meshdad]].
* **Account:** The magic lamp found by Malik is an object of great power. It is said to grant three wishes to any who find it and use it with wisdom. The lamp is preserved in the Grand Market of Meshdad, where it draws visitors from across Marethar.

#### The Temple of Thalaran

* **Origin:** A sacred site of [[Thalassians|Thalassia]].
* **Account:** The Temple of Thalaran stands on the coast of Thalassia, dedicated to [[Spirits of Thalaran|Thalaran]], the Spirit of the Sea. Every year, sailors come here to ask for protection and blessing before setting out on their voyages. The temple is known for its carvings and for the ritual ceremonies held within its walls.

#### The Crown of Shahin

* **Origin:** A sacred artifact of [[Aridonians|Aridonia]].
* **Account:** [[The Crown of Shahin]] is a legendary artifact said to confer wisdom and power upon whoever wears it. It is kept in the Sanctuary of Shahin, at the heart of the Aridonian desert, and is held to be a symbol of leadership and divine protection.
