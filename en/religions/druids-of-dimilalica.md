# Druidic Orders of Dimilalica

*The cycle that breathes beneath the stone*

Dimilalica is not a deity. It is a concept, a system, a way of perceiving **the hidden symmetry in seasons, animals, minerals**. Its druids seek no power: they seek balance. But in Marethar, every balance is also political tension.

The official doctrine is widespread among rural cultures and some autonomous communities of the interior. Druids live in mobile groups, interpret signs of the wind, read cracks in the earth, and **preserve rituals in oral form, often in encoded verse**.

Many of them refuse writing. The so-called **Apocrypha of Dimilalica** hold that every piece of transcribed knowledge loses its power. Others attempt an "emergency codification," fearing the knowledge will be lost.

> "He who writes a prayer, kills it. But he who does not pass it on, buries it."
> — apocryphal motto handed down among the forests of Velhan

In recent times, some druids have aligned with radical movements, opposing public works, dams, and magical academies. But **no one can say where faith ends and guerrilla begins**.

## Extended Profile

**Type:** Cult
**Form:** Nature Cult
**Culture:** Aurelian
**Area:** Aurelia
**Faithful:** 2,330

**Description:** The Druids of Dimilalica venerate Dimilalica, the Summer Jaguar. This religion centers on the nature cult and on seasonal cycles, with rituals celebrating the sun, the earth, and the seasons. The faithful believe that Dimilalica guides the cycle of life and offers prosperity and protection during times of abundance.

**Origins and Religious Practices:** The religion of the Druids of Dimilalica originates in the ancient forests and meadows of the Aurelians, where the earliest inhabitants lived in close harmony with nature. Rituals include ritual dances, offerings of fruit and flowers, and celebrations at the solstices and equinoxes. Ceremonies are often held in sacred clearings surrounded by ancient trees.

**Clergy Structure:** The druids are the spiritual leaders of this religion, respected for their knowledge of herbs, wildlife, and natural cycles. They lead the rituals, offer counsel to the community, and teach the secrets of nature to young initiates.
