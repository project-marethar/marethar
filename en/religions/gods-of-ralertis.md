# Gods of Ralertis

*The Sapphire Reaper and the discipline of immortality*

Ralertis is ordered power. It is the name most spoken in imperial courts, in Aurelian temples, in secular tribunals. It is not a god of grace or clemency, but of **form, measure, and final perfection**.

It is said that those who die in the name of Ralertis are not reborn, but **become eternal principle**: a note inscribed in the Cosmos. For the Aurelians, this is desirable.

Its cult is hierarchical, with rites marked by ceremonial clocks, geometric dances, and sacred readings in dead languages. The faithful wear **symbols sewn inside their garments, never visible**, as a sign of inner order.

Ralertis guides judges, architects, philosophers, but also ritual assassins and purifiers. Its temples are **towers empty at the center**, as a reminder that only the void has perfect form.

> "He who leaves shadows in his corner does not deserve the temple."
> — moral code of the Brotherhood of Ralertis (eastern section)

Cyclical heresies maintain that Ralertis is **a demon of inertia, not a god**, and that perfection kills. These movements are suppressed with speed and discretion.

## Extended Profile

**Type:** Organized
**Form:** Polytheism
**Culture:** Aurelian
**Area:** Aurelia
**Faithful:** 1,367,240

**Description:** The religion of the Gods of Ralertis venerates Ralertis, the Sapphire Reaper, and a pantheon of lesser deities. The faithful participate in complex rituals and celebrations honoring these deities for prosperity and protection. The religion emphasizes harmony between the gods and human beings, with rites celebrating the cycles of nature and human life.

**Origins and Religious Practices:** This religion has its origins in the ancient Aurelian civilizations, which believed in a pantheon of deities responsible for various aspects of life and nature. Rituals include offerings of jewels, fruits, and animals, alongside elaborate ceremonies involving chants, dances, and recitations of ancient myths. The principal festivities take place at the seasonal transitions and significant astronomical events.

**Clergy Structure:** The religion is guided by a well-structured clergy of priests and priestesses who serve in the temples dedicated to Ralertis and the lesser deities. They are responsible for performing the rituals, managing the temples, and providing the faithful with religious education.
